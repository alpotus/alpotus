IRON PREPARATIONS

Iron preparations are used mainly for:
  • Treatment of iron deficiency anaemia
  • Prevention of iron deficiency
  • Iron can be administered:
    • Orally
    • Parenterally

IMPORTANCE OF IRON

  • Iron is essential for:
    • Hemoglobin synthesis
    • Oxygen transport
    • Normal cellular metabolism
FLOWCHART: Deficiency of iron -> Reduced hemoglobin synthesis -> Microcytic hypochromic anaemia -> Reduced oxygen carrying capacity of blood -> Tissue hypoxia -> Symptoms such as: • Weakness • Fatigue • Headache • Irritability • Reduced exercise tolerance

CLASSIFICATION OF IRON PREPARATIONS

ORAL PREPARATIONS

  • Ferrous sulphate
  • Ferrous fumarate
  • Ferrous gluconate
  • Ferric ammonium citrate
  • Colloidal ferric hydroxide
  • Carbonyl iron

PARENTERAL PREPARATIONS

  • Iron dextran
  • Ferrous sucrose
  • Ferric carboxymaltose
  • Iron isomaltoside

ORAL IRON PREPARATIONS

  • Oral iron therapy is:
    • Convenient
    • Cheap
    • Commonly used
  • The important factor is:
    • Amount of elemental iron present in the preparation
  • because therapeutic response depends on elemental iron content.

COMMON ORAL PREPARATIONS

TABLE: | Preparation | Elemental Iron Content | Important Points | | Ferrous sulphate | 20% (hydrated), 32% (dried) | Oldest and cheapest preparation | | Ferrous fumarate | 33% | Higher elemental iron content | | Ferrous gluconate | 12% | Lower elemental iron content | | Colloidal ferric hydroxide | 50% | High elemental iron | | Carbonyl iron | Highly purified metallic iron | Better purity |

HOW ORAL IRON WORKS

FLOWCHART: Oral iron administration -> Absorption from intestine -> Iron enters bloodstream -> Transport to bone marrow -> Hemoglobin synthesis increases -> RBC production increases -> Correction of anaemia

HOW TO TAKE ORAL IRON

  • Oral iron is preferably given:
    • After food

REASON

  • Minimizes gastric irritation

ADVERSE EFFECTS OF ORAL IRON

  • Common gastrointestinal adverse effects:
    • Dyspepsia
    • Metallic taste
    • Constipation
    • Diarrhoea
    • Gastric discomfort

LIQUID IRON PREPARATIONS

  • Liquid iron preparations may:
    • Stain teeth

PREVENTION

  • Drink water after administration

PARENTERAL IRON PREPARATIONS

  • Parenteral iron therapy is used when:
    • Oral iron is not tolerated
    • Iron is not absorbed properly
    • Severe deficiency is present
    • Rapid replenishment of stores is needed

INDICATIONS FOR PARENTERAL IRON THERAPY

  • 1. Intolerance to oral iron
  • 2. Malabsorption of iron
  • 3. Noncompliance to oral iron
  • 4. Severe iron deficiency
  • 5. Chronic kidney disease patients receiving erythropoietin

WHY PARENTERAL IRON IS USED

FLOWCHART: Oral iron ineffective / not tolerated -> Need for adequate iron replacement -> Parenteral iron therapy preferred

CALCULATION OF TOTAL IRON REQUIREMENT

  • Iron requirement depends on:
    • Body weight
    • Hemoglobin deficit
    • Iron stores

FORMULA

Iron requirement (mg) = 2.4 × Body weight (kg) × Hb deficit (g/dL) + (500–1000)
  • Extra 500–1000 mg is added:
    • To replenish iron stores

NORMAL HEMOGLOBIN VALUES

TABLE: | Group | Normal Hb | | Men | 14–16 g/dL | | Women | 12–14 g/dL |

INTRAMUSCULAR IRON THERAPY

PREPARATION USED

  • Iron dextran complex

DOSE

  • 100 mg daily (2 mL) OR
  • On alternate days
  • until total required dose is completed.

MAXIMUM DOSE

  • 2 g

Z-TRACK TECHNIQUE

FLOWCHART: Skin and subcutaneous tissue pulled to one side -> Intramuscular injection given -> Track sealed after needle removal -> Prevents leakage of iron -> Prevents skin staining

INTRAVENOUS IRON THERAPY

1. IRON DEXTRAN

FEATURES

  • Low molecular weight preparation
  • Can be given as total dose infusion

METHOD

FLOWCHART: Calculated total iron dose -> Diluted in normal saline -> Slow intravenous infusion over 6–8 hours -> Iron stores replenished

IMPORTANT POINT

  • Test dose required
  • Constant supervision required

2. FERROUS SUCROSE

FEATURES

  • Given as multiple IV infusions
  • Useful in chronic kidney disease

3. FERRIC CARBOXYMALTOSE

FEATURES

  • Iron hydroxide complex
  • Iron bound to carbohydrate

ADMINISTRATION

  • IV infusion in normal saline

4. IRON ISOMALTOSIDE

FEATURES

  • Tightly bound iron preparation
  • Slow release of free iron
  • Single IV infusion possible
  • Convenient for patients
  • Allergic reactions are rare

TEST DOSE REQUIREMENT

TEST DOSE REQUIRED

  • Iron dextran

USUALLY NOT REQUIRED

  • Ferrous sucrose
  • Ferric carboxymaltose
  • Iron isomaltoside
  • unless patient has:
    • History of drug allergy

IMPORTANT PRECAUTION DURING IV IRON THERAPY

FLOWCHART: Intravenous iron infusion started -> Patient monitored carefully -> Early detection of hypersensitivity reaction -> Immediate management possible

ADVERSE EFFECTS OF PARENTERAL IRON

LOCAL ADVERSE EFFECTS (IM INJECTION)

  • Pain
  • Abscess formation
  • Skin discoloration

SYSTEMIC ADVERSE EFFECTS

  • Headache
  • Fever
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Arthralgia
  • Lymphadenopathy
  • Urticaria
  • Circulatory collapse

SEVERE ADVERSE EFFECT

  • Anaphylactoid reaction
  • More common with:
    • Older iron preparations
  • Therefore:
    • Resuscitation facilities should always be available

THERAPEUTIC USES OF IRON

1. TREATMENT OF IRON DEFICIENCY ANAEMIA

CAUSES

  • Pregnancy
  • Blood loss
  • Nutritional deficiency
  • Poor absorption from gut

FEATURES OF IRON DEFICIENCY ANAEMIA

  • Weakness
  • Fatigue
  • Headache
  • Irritability
  • Reduced exercise tolerance

COMMON ORAL REGIMEN

  • Ferrous sulphate
  • 200 mg ferrous sulphate
  • Contains 60 mg elemental iron
  • Given three times daily after food

RESPONSE TO IRON THERAPY

FLOWCHART: Iron therapy started -> Bone marrow activity increases -> Reticulocyte count rises -> Hemoglobin level increases -> Symptoms improve

EXPECTED RISE IN Hb

  • 0.7–1 g/dL/week

INITIALLY

  • Hb rises rapidly

LATER

  • Rise becomes slower

DURATION OF THERAPY

FLOWCHART: Hemoglobin becomes normal -> Body iron stores may still be depleted -> Iron therapy continued for 3–6 months -> Iron stores replenished -> Relapse prevented

ORAL vs PARENTERAL RESPONSE

  • Parenteral iron:
    • Does NOT raise Hb faster than oral iron

BUT

  • Replenishes iron stores more rapidly

2. PROPHYLAXIS OF IRON DEFICIENCY

  • Iron prophylaxis is useful during:
    • Pregnancy
    • Infancy

WHY IRON IS REQUIRED DURING PREGNANCY

FLOWCHART: Pregnancy -> Increased iron requirement -> Requirement for: • Growing fetus • Enlarging uterus • Increased maternal blood volume • Blood loss during labor -> Iron supplementation required

PROPHYLACTIC REGIMEN

IRON

  • 100 mg elemental iron daily
  • Started from second trimester

FOLIC ACID

  • 0.5 mg/day
  • Started from first trimester

PURPOSE

  • Prevent neural tube defects

ACUTE IRON POISONING

  • Usually seen in:
    • Young children

PATHOGENESIS OF ACUTE IRON POISONING

FLOWCHART: Excess iron ingestion -> Direct corrosive injury to GI mucosa -> Vomiting + abdominal pain + bloody diarrhea -> Iron absorbed into bloodstream -> Metabolic acidosis + cellular toxicity -> Shock, convulsions, coma -> Death in severe cases

CLINICAL FEATURES OF ACUTE IRON POISONING

  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Epigastric pain
  • Bloody diarrhoea
  • Dehydration
  • Cyanosis
  • Drowsiness
  • Hyperventilation
  • Metabolic acidosis
  • Convulsions
  • Coma
  • Death

TREATMENT OF ACUTE IRON POISONING

GENERAL MEASURES

SUPPORTIVE CARE

  • Maintain:
    • Airway
    • Breathing
    • Circulation
    • Fluid balance
    • Electrolyte balance

GASTRIC DECONTAMINATION

FLOWCHART: Iron present in stomach -> Vomiting induced / gastric lavage performed -> Sodium bicarbonate precipitates iron -> Iron absorption reduced

CONTROL OF CONVULSIONS

  • IV diazepam slowly

SPECIFIC THERAPY

DEFEROXAMINE (DESFERRIOXAMINE)

MECHANISM

FLOWCHART: Deferoxamine administration -> Chelates free iron -> Forms water-soluble complex -> Complex excreted in urine -> Iron toxicity decreases

ROUTE

  • IV infusion OR
  • Intramuscular

OTHER CHELATORS

DEFERIPRONE

  • Oral iron chelator
  • Less effective in acute poisoning

CALCIUM EDETATE

  • Also useful in iron poisoning
