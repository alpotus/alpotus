Angiotensin receptor blockers are important antihypertensive drugs that directly block the action of angiotensin II at its receptor. They are especially useful in patients who cannot tolerate ACE inhibitors because of dry cough or angioedema.
TABLE: | Drug Group | Drugs Included | | Angiotensin Receptor Blockers (ARBs) | Losartan, Candesartan, Irbesartan, Valsartan, Telmisartan, Olmesartan, Eprosartan |

Mechanism of Action

ARBs act mainly as highly selective competitive antagonists at the AT1 receptor.

TABLE: | Property | Explanation | | Main receptor blocked | AT1 receptor | | Receptor selectivity | Highly selective for AT1 over AT2 receptors | | Example of selectivity | Losartan is nearly 10,000 times more selective for AT1 receptors | | Effect on bradykinin | No effect on bradykinin degradation | | Result | Vasodilation, reduced aldosterone secretion and fall in blood pressure |

The normal actions of angiotensin II occur through AT1 receptors.

FLOWCHART: Angiotensin II is produced in the circulation -> Angiotensin II binds to the AT1 receptor -> AT1 receptor activation produces vasoconstriction -> Aldosterone secretion and sodium retention increase -> Sympathetic activity increases -> Blood pressure rises

ARBs prevent all these actions by blocking the AT1 receptor.

FLOWCHART: ARB occupies the AT1 receptor -> Angiotensin II cannot bind to the receptor -> Vasoconstriction does not occur -> Aldosterone secretion falls -> Sodium and water retention decrease -> Blood pressure falls

ARBs provide more complete blockade than ACE inhibitors because they prevent the action of angiotensin II irrespective of how it is formed.

FLOWCHART: Angiotensin II can be formed through the ACE pathway -> Angiotensin II can also be formed through alternative non-ACE pathways -> ACE inhibitors block only the ACE pathway -> Some angiotensin II may still be available FLOWCHART: ARB blocks the receptor directly -> All actions of angiotensin II are prevented -> The antihypertensive effect becomes more complete

ARBs also indirectly increase stimulation of AT2 receptors.

FLOWCHART: AT1 receptors normally suppress further renin release -> ARB blocks the AT1 receptor -> Renin secretion increases -> More angiotensin II is formed -> The excess angiotensin II stimulates the unblocked AT2 receptor -> AT2 receptor activation causes vasodilation and inhibits cell growth -> Additional blood pressure lowering occurs

Unlike ACE inhibitors, ARBs do not alter bradykinin metabolism.

FLOWCHART: ACE inhibitor prevents breakdown of bradykinin -> Bradykinin accumulates in the lungs -> Dry cough and angioedema may occur FLOWCHART: ARB does not interfere with bradykinin degradation -> Bradykinin concentration remains normal -> Dry cough and angioedema are uncommon

Pharmacological Actions

Cardiovascular Effects

ARBs block the vasoconstrictor effect of angiotensin II on blood vessels.

FLOWCHART: ARB blocks AT1 receptors in arterioles and venules -> Blood vessels relax and dilate -> Total peripheral resistance decreases -> Blood pressure falls -> Heart rate and cardiovascular reflexes remain largely unchanged

Renal Effects

ARBs reduce sodium and water retention by blocking the actions of angiotensin II and aldosterone.

FLOWCHART: Angiotensin II normally stimulates sodium reabsorption in the kidney -> Angiotensin II also stimulates aldosterone release from the adrenal cortex -> ARB blocks these actions -> Less sodium and water are retained -> Natriuresis and diuresis occur -> Blood pressure falls further

Effect on Cardiac and Vascular Remodeling

FLOWCHART: Angiotensin II stimulates growth of cardiac muscle and vascular smooth muscle -> Long-standing stimulation produces ventricular hypertrophy and vascular hypertrophy -> ARB blocks the growth-promoting action of angiotensin II -> Ventricular and vascular hypertrophy regress -> Cardiac remodeling improves

Certain ARBs possess additional unique effects.

TABLE: | Drug | Special Effect | | Losartan | Produces a mild uricosuric effect similar to probenecid and may lower serum uric acid | | Eprosartan | May reduce norepinephrine release from sympathetic nerves more prominently |

Pharmacokinetics

The pharmacokinetic properties vary among different ARBs.

TABLE: | Drug | Absorption and Food Effect | Active Metabolite | Special Feature | | Losartan | Oral bioavailability about 33%; food does not affect absorption | E3174 present | E3174 is 10–30 times more potent than losartan | | Valsartan | Food reduces absorption | No significant active metabolite | Absorption decreases when taken with food | | Irbesartan | High oral bioavailability | No significant active metabolite | Well absorbed orally | | Telmisartan | Well absorbed; food has little effect | No active metabolite | Half-life more than 24 hours | | Eprosartan | Moderate absorption | No active metabolite | Does not form active metabolites |

Losartan undergoes metabolism in the liver to produce a highly active metabolite.

FLOWCHART: Losartan is absorbed from the gastrointestinal tract -> Losartan reaches the liver -> Part of the drug is converted into the metabolite E3174 -> E3174 is much more potent than losartan -> The metabolite contributes significantly to the antihypertensive effect

Most ARBs are highly bound to plasma proteins.

TABLE: | Drug | Plasma Protein Binding | | Losartan | Approximately 98% | | Most other ARBs | Highly protein bound |

Most ARBs are eliminated through the kidneys and bile.

TABLE: | Drug | Route of Elimination | | Losartan | Kidney and bile | | Telmisartan | Mainly through bile | | Most other ARBs | Kidney and bile |

Because telmisartan has a very long half-life, it can be administered once daily.

Adverse Effects

ARBs are generally very well tolerated and usually produce fewer side effects than ACE inhibitors.

TABLE: | Adverse Effect | Explanation | | Hypotension | May occur especially in volume-depleted patients | | Hyperkalemia | Due to reduced aldosterone secretion | | Headache | Common mild complaint | | Dizziness | Due to fall in blood pressure | | Weakness | Mild generalized weakness may occur | | Upper gastrointestinal upset | Nausea or abdominal discomfort may occur | | Dry cough | Rare because bradykinin is not increased | | Angioedema | Rare compared with ACE inhibitors |

Hyperkalemia develops because aldosterone secretion is reduced. Refer Last for more

FLOWCHART: ARB blocks the effect of angiotensin II on the adrenal cortex -> Aldosterone secretion decreases -> Potassium excretion by the kidney decreases -> Serum potassium concentration rises -> Hyperkalemia may develop

The most serious toxicity is fetotoxicity.

FLOWCHART: ARB is taken during pregnancy -> The fetal renin-angiotensin system becomes suppressed -> Normal development of the fetal kidney is impaired -> Oligohydramnios and growth retardation occur -> Renal tubular dysgenesis and neonatal renal failure may occur

Fetopathic Effects of ARBs

  • Oligohydramnios
  • Intrauterine growth retardation
  • Renal tubular dysgenesis
  • Neonatal renal failure

Contraindications

TABLE: | Contraindication | Reason | | Pregnancy | Strictly contraindicated because of proven fetal toxicity | | Bilateral renal artery stenosis | Acute renal failure may occur | | Single functioning kidney with renal artery stenosis | Severe renal failure may occur | | Hepatic dysfunction | Dose reduction may be required with losartan and telmisartan |

The reason for avoiding ARBs in renal artery stenosis is similar to that for ACE inhibitors.

FLOWCHART: Renal artery stenosis decreases blood supply to the kidney -> Angiotensin II normally constricts the efferent arteriole -> Glomerular filtration pressure is maintained FLOWCHART: ARB blocks the action of angiotensin II -> The efferent arteriole dilates -> Glomerular filtration pressure falls -> Acute renal failure may occur

Drug Interactions

TABLE: | Combination | Result | | ARB + Potassium-sparing diuretic | Severe hyperkalemia may occur | | ARB + Potassium supplements | Hyperkalemia may occur | | ARB + ACE inhibitor | Excessive hyperkalemia and renal dysfunction may occur | | ARB + NSAIDs | Antihypertensive effect decreases | | ARB + Lithium | Lithium toxicity may occur |

The interaction with NSAIDs reduces the antihypertensive action of ARBs.

FLOWCHART: NSAID is taken together with an ARB -> NSAID inhibits renal prostaglandin synthesis -> Sodium and water retention increase -> The blood pressure lowering effect of the ARB becomes weaker

Lithium toxicity may develop because ARBs reduce its renal elimination.

FLOWCHART: ARB reduces the clearance of lithium by the kidney -> Lithium accumulates in the body -> Symptoms of lithium toxicity appear

Therapeutic Uses

TABLE: | Clinical Condition | Benefit of ARBs | | Hypertension | First-line drugs for all grades of hypertension | | Hypertension with ACE inhibitor induced cough | Preferred because they rarely produce cough | | Congestive heart failure | Improve symptoms and survival | | Diabetic nephropathy | Reduce proteinuria and delay progression of renal disease | | Post-myocardial infarction | Improve survival and prevent ventricular remodeling | | Chronic kidney disease | Reduce proteinuria and slow disease progression |

ARBs are especially useful in patients who cannot tolerate ACE inhibiors.

FLOWCHART: ACE inhibitor is prescribed for hypertension -> Persistent dry cough develops -> ACE inhibitor is stopped -> ARB is started instead -> Blood pressure remains controlled without cough

ARBs are also important in diabetic nephropathy.

FLOWCHART: Diabetes damages the renal glomerulus and increases intraglomerular pressure -> ARB dilates the efferent arteriole -> Intraglomerular pressure decreases -> Proteinuria decreases -> Progression to end-stage renal disease slows

Following myocardial infarction, ARBs reduce harmful cardiac remodeling.

FLOWCHART: Myocardial infarction damages the left ventricle -> Angiotensin II promotes ventricular enlargement and remodeling -> ARB blocks the effect of angiotensin II -> Ventricular remodeling slows -> Cardiac function and survival improve
