SEDATIVES AND HYPNOTICS

INTRODUCTION

  • The brain normally maintains a balance between:
    • Excitatory activity → keeps us awake, alert, active
    • Inhibitory activity → produces calmness, relaxation, and sleep

PROGRESSION OF CNS DEPRESSION

FLOWCHART: Mild CNS depression -> Calmness and reduction of anxiety -> Sedation FLOWCHART: Further CNS depression -> Sleep production -> Hypnosis FLOWCHART: Excessive CNS depression -> Unconsciousness -> Coma -> Death

IMPORTANT DEFINITIONS

  • Sedatives calm the person without necessarily producing sleep.
  • Hypnotics produce sleep resembling normal sleep.
  • Most sedative–hypnotic drugs work by enhancing inhibitory neurotransmission in the CNS.

SLEEP PHYSIOLOGY

1. NON-RAPID EYE MOVEMENT (NREM) SLEEP

  • NREM sleep consists of stages:
    • Stage 0 → Awake state
    • Stage 1 → Light sleep
    • Stage 2 → Major part of normal sleep
    • Stage 3 and 4 → Deep slow-wave sleep
  • About 50% of total sleep time is spent in stage 2.
  • Slow-wave sleep (Stages 3 and 4) is important for:
    • Physical restoration
    • Relaxation
    • Feeling refreshed

2. RAPID EYE MOVEMENT (REM) SLEEP

  • REM sleep is associated with:
    • Dreaming
    • Rapid eye movements
    • Increased brain activity
  • REM sleep constitutes about 20–30% of total sleep time.
  • Normal sleep cycles alternate between NREM and REM sleep.
  • Good hypnotic drugs should produce sleep close to normal physiological sleep.

SLEEP DISORDERS

INSOMNIA

  • Difficulty in:
    • Falling asleep
    • Maintaining sleep
    • Getting refreshing sleep

TYPES

FLOWCHART: Transient insomnia (< 3 days) -> Usually due to stress, travel, excitement FLOWCHART: Short-term insomnia (3 days–3 weeks) -> Often due to illness or emotional stress FLOWCHART: Long-term insomnia (> 3 weeks) -> Needs evaluation for psychiatric or medical causes

TREATMENT

  • Sedatives
  • Hypnotics
  • Sleep hygiene measures

HYPERSOMNIA (NARCOLEPSY)

  • Characterized by excessive daytime sleepiness.

TREATMENT

  • CNS stimulants
  • Modafinil
  • Amphetamine-like drugs

BENZODIAZEPINES (BZDs)

BASIC STRUCTURE

FLOWCHART: Benzene ring + Seven-membered diazepine ring

MECHANISM OF ACTION OF BENZODIAZEPINES

ROLE OF GABA IN BRAIN

  • GABA is the major inhibitory neurotransmitter of the CNS.
FLOWCHART: GABA receptor activated -> Chloride channels open -> Chloride enters neuron -> Neuron becomes hyperpolarized -> Neuron becomes less excitable -> CNS activity decreases

HOW BZDs WORK

  • BZDs do NOT directly activate GABA receptors.
FLOWCHART: BZDs bind to specific benzodiazepine site on GABA-A receptor -> Increase frequency of chloride channel opening -> Enhance action of GABA -> More chloride enters neuron -> Neuronal activity suppressed -> CNS depression occurs

THEREFORE BZDs PRODUCE

  • Calmness
  • Sleep
  • Anxiolysis
  • Muscle relaxation
  • Anticonvulsant effect

SITES OF ACTION OF BZDs

  • Limbic system → anxiolytic effect
  • Reticular activating system → sedation and sleep
  • Spinal cord → muscle relaxation
  • Cerebral cortex → anticonvulsant action

PHARMACOLOGICAL ACTIONS AND USES OF BZDs

1. SEDATIVE AND HYPNOTIC ACTION

FLOWCHART: Reduce sleep latency (time required to fall asleep) -> Increase total sleep duration -> Reduce night awakenings -> Produce more refreshing sleep

EFFECT ON SLEEP STAGES

  • Stage 2 NREM sleep increases
  • Deep slow-wave sleep slightly decreases
  • REM sleep decreases moderately
  • Compared to barbiturates, sleep produced by BZDs is more similar to normal sleep.

WHY BZDs ARE PREFERRED OVER BARBITURATES

FLOWCHART: Wide therapeutic index -> Lower risk of fatal respiratory depression FLOWCHART: Less disturbance of sleep architecture -> Less rebound insomnia FLOWCHART: No enzyme induction -> Fewer drug interactions FLOWCHART: Specific antidote available (flumazenil) -> Safer in overdose

2. ANTIANXIETY EFFECT

  • Anxiety occurs due to excessive neuronal activity in the limbic system.
  • BZDs suppress this excessive activity.

HENCE THEY PRODUCE

  • Relaxation
  • Emotional calmness
  • Reduction of fear and tension

COMMONLY USED DRUGS

  • Diazepam
  • Alprazolam
  • Lorazepam
  • Oxazepam
  • BZDs act rapidly and are useful in acute anxiety.

3. ANTICONVULSANT EFFECT

  • Excessive neuronal firing causes seizures.
  • BZDs increase inhibitory GABA activity and suppress abnormal neuronal discharge.

USES

FLOWCHART: Status epilepticus -> IV diazepam or lorazepam FLOWCHART: Absence seizures -> Clonazepam FLOWCHART: Febrile convulsions -> Diazepam

4. MUSCLE RELAXANT ACTION

FLOWCHART: BZDs suppress polysynaptic reflexes in the spinal cord -> Reduced skeletal muscle tone -> Muscle relaxation

USEFUL IN

  • Muscle spasm
  • Cerebral palsy
  • Spinal injury
  • Tetanus

5. PRE-ANAESTHETIC MEDICATION

  • Before surgery, BZDs are given because they:
    • Reduce anxiety
    • Produce sedation
    • Cause amnesia of perioperative events

DRUGS COMMONLY USED

  • Midazolam
  • Diazepam
  • Lorazepam

6. CONSCIOUS SEDATION

  • In procedures like:
    • Endoscopy
    • Minor surgery
    • Dental procedures
FLOWCHART: Relaxation -> Drowsiness -> Patient remains conscious but calm and cooperative

7. ALCOHOL WITHDRAWAL

  • Alcohol withdrawal causes CNS hyperexcitability.
  • BZDs suppress this excessive CNS activity.

PREFERRED DRUGS

  • Diazepam
  • Chlordiazepoxide

PHARMACOKINETICS OF BZDs

ABSORPTION

  • Most BZDs are:
    • Well absorbed orally
    • Lipid soluble
  • IV route is used in emergencies.
  • IM absorption is unreliable for many drugs except lorazepam.

DISTRIBUTION

  • BZDs are highly lipid soluble.
FLOWCHART: Rapid entry into brain -> Rapid onset of action
  • They also cross:
    • Placenta
    • Breast milk

METABOLISM

  • Most BZDs are metabolized in liver.
  • Some produce active metabolites.

EXAMPLES

  • Diazepam
  • Alprazolam
  • Chlordiazepoxide
  • These active metabolites prolong drug action.

OXAZEPAM AND LORAZEPAM

FLOWCHART: Do not form active metabolites -> Safer in elderly -> Safer in liver disease

ADVERSE EFFECTS OF BZDs

COMMON ADVERSE EFFECTS

FLOWCHART: Excess CNS depression -> Drowsiness Confusion Poor concentration Reduced alertness

OTHER EFFECTS

  • Blurred vision
  • Ataxia
  • Amnesia
  • Disorientation

TOLERANCE AND DEPENDENCE

FLOWCHART: Repeated exposure to drug -> Brain adapts to drug -> Tolerance develops
  • Higher doses become necessary to produce same effect.
  • Dependence may also occur.

WITHDRAWAL SYMPTOMS

FLOWCHART: Sudden loss of CNS inhibition -> CNS hyperexcitability

SYMPTOMS

  • Anxiety
  • Tremors
  • Insomnia
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability
  • Convulsions
  • Therefore BZDs should be tapered gradually.

FLOPPY BABY SYNDROME

FLOWCHART: Drug crosses placenta -> CNS depression in newborn -> Hypotonia + respiratory depression
  • This is called floppy baby syndrome.

PARADOXICAL REACTIONS

  • Rarely BZDs may produce:
    • Agitation
    • Anxiety
    • Excitement
    • Aggression
    • Convulsions
  • instead of sedation.

FLUMAZENIL

WHAT IS FLUMAZENIL?

  • Flumazenil is a benzodiazepine receptor antagonist.

MECHANISM

FLOWCHART: Flumazenil competes with BZDs for benzodiazepine receptor -> Blocks action of BZDs -> Reverses CNS depression

USES

  • Benzodiazepine overdose
  • Reversal of sedation after anaesthesia

ADVERSE EFFECTS

  • Nausea
  • Dizziness
  • Confusion
FLOWCHART: Sudden reversal of BZD action -> Withdrawal symptoms -> Anxiety or seizures

IMPORTANT BENZODIAZEPINES

DIAZEPAM

FEATURES

  • Prototype BZD
  • Rapid onset
  • Produces active metabolites
  • Long duration

USES

  • Anxiety
  • Status epilepticus
  • Alcohol withdrawal
  • Muscle spasm

IMPORTANT POINT

FLOWCHART: Brain concentration falls quickly -> Patient wakes up earlier
  • Even though elimination half-life is long.

LORAZEPAM

FEATURES

  • No active metabolites
  • Less affected by liver disease
  • Longer anticonvulsant action

USES

  • Status epilepticus
  • Anxiety
  • Preanaesthetic medication

ALPRAZOLAM

FEATURES

  • Strong anxiolytic effect
  • Also has antidepressant action

USES

  • Panic disorder
  • Anxiety disorders

MIDAZOLAM

FEATURES

  • Very rapid onset
  • Short acting

USES

  • IV sedation
  • Endoscopy
  • Anaesthesia induction

NITRAZEPAM AND TEMAZEPAM

  • Mainly used for insomnia.

BARBITURATES

MECHANISM

  • Barbiturates also enhance GABA action.
  • But unlike BZDs:
FLOWCHART: Increase duration of chloride channel opening -> Produce much stronger CNS depression
  • At high doses they can directly activate GABA receptors.
  • Hence they are much more dangerous.

WHY BARBITURATES ARE DANGEROUS

FLOWCHART: Strong CNS depression -> Respiratory depression -> Coma and death in overdose
  • They also:
    • Cause tolerance rapidly
    • Produce dependence
    • Induce liver enzymes
    • Cause many drug interactions
  • Therefore they are rarely used now as hypnotics.

NON-BENZODIAZEPINE HYPNOTICS

DRUGS

  • Zolpidem
  • Zopiclone
  • Zaleplon
  • Eszopiclone
  • These drugs act on benzodiazepine receptors but mainly produce sleep.

ADVANTAGES

FLOWCHART: Less disturbance of sleep architecture -> Less daytime sedation -> Less dependence

MAIN USE

  • Insomnia

MELATONIN

  • Melatonin is a hormone secreted by pineal gland.
  • It regulates circadian rhythm.

USED IN

  • Jet lag
  • Sleep disorders
  • Ramelteon acts on melatonin receptors.

SUVOREXANT

  • Suvorexant blocks orexin receptors.
  • Orexin normally promotes wakefulness.
FLOWCHART: Orexin blocked -> Wakefulness decreases -> Sleep occurs
  • Used in insomnia.
