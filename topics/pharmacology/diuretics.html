POTASSIUM-SPARING DIURETICS

INTRODUCTION

Potassium-sparing diuretics are weak diuretics that increase sodium excretion while retaining potassium.
  • Unlike loop and thiazide diuretics, they do not produce potassium loss. Hence they are called potassium-sparing diuretics.
  • They mainly act on:
    • Late distal tubule
    • Collecting duct
  • These nephron segments normally regulate:
    • Sodium reabsorption
    • Potassium secretion

CLASSIFICATION

1. ALDOSTERONE ANTAGONISTS

  • Spironolactone
  • Eplerenone
  • These drugs block aldosterone action.

2. DIRECTLY ACTING POTASSIUM-SPARING DIURETICS

  • Amiloride
  • Triamterene
  • These drugs directly block sodium channels.

ALDOSTERONE ANTAGONISTS

SPIRONOLACTONE

INTRODUCTION

  • Spironolactone is a competitive antagonist of aldosterone.
  • It blocks:
    • Mineralocorticoid receptor (MR)
  • It is most effective when aldosterone levels are high.
  • Therefore particularly useful in:
    • Congestive cardiac failure
    • Hepatic cirrhosis
    • Nephrotic syndrome
    • Hyperaldosteronism

NORMAL ACTION OF ALDOSTERONE

  • Normally aldosterone acts on distal nephron and causes:
    • Increased sodium reabsorption
    • Increased water retention
    • Increased potassium excretion
  • This occurs by increasing synthesis of aldosterone-induced proteins inside tubular cells.

MECHANISM OF ACTION OF SPIRONOLACTONE

FLOWCHART: Aldosterone binds mineralocorticoid receptor -> Formation of aldosterone-induced proteins -> Na+ reabsorption increases -> Water retention increases -> K+ excretion increases FLOWCHART: Spironolactone blocks mineralocorticoid receptor -> Formation of aldosterone-induced proteins decreases -> Na+ reabsorption decreases -> Water excretion increases -> K+ excretion decreases

NET EFFECTS

  • Sodium excretion increases
  • Water excretion increases
  • Potassium is retained
  • Spironolactone also increases calcium excretion.

PHARMACOKINETICS

  • Administered orally
  • Partly absorbed from GIT
  • Highly bound to plasma proteins
  • Extensively metabolized in liver
  • Forms active metabolite: canrenone
  • Canrenone has long half-life

THERAPEUTIC USES

1. OEDEMATOUS STATES WITH SECONDARY HYPERALDOSTERONISM

USEFUL IN

  • Congestive cardiac failure
  • Hepatic cirrhosis
  • Nephrotic syndrome

WHY USEFUL

FLOWCHART: Secondary hyperaldosteronism -> Na+ and water retention -> Oedema develops FLOWCHART: Spironolactone blocks aldosterone action -> Na+ and water retention decrease -> Oedema improves

2. CONGESTIVE CARDIAC FAILURE (CCF)

  • Spironolactone is important in moderate to severe heart failure.

BENEFITS IN HEART FAILURE

FLOWCHART: Blocks aldosterone effects -> ↓ Na+ and water retention -> ↓ Cardiac preload and congestion -> Improvement in heart failure symptoms FLOWCHART: Blocks aldosterone-mediated myocardial fibrosis -> ↓ Ventricular remodelling -> Slows progression of heart failure FLOWCHART: ↓ K+ loss -> Prevents hypokalaemia

3. COMBINATION WITH LOOP OR THIAZIDE DIURETICS

  • Loop and thiazide diuretics commonly cause potassium loss.
  • Spironolactone conserves potassium.

BENEFIT OF COMBINATION

FLOWCHART: Loop/thiazide diuretics -> Cause K+ loss -> Hypokalaemia develops FLOWCHART: Spironolactone -> Retains K+ -> Corrects hypokalaemia
  • Combination therapy also:
    • Improves diuretic response
    • Enhances antihypertensive effect

4. RESISTANT HYPERTENSION

  • Especially useful in:
    • Primary hyperaldosteronism (Conn’s syndrome)

ADVERSE EFFECTS

1. HYPERKALAEMIA

  • Most important adverse effect.

MECHANISM

FLOWCHART: K+ excretion decreases -> Serum potassium increases -> Hyperkalaemia develops

RISK IS HIGHER IN

  • Renal disease
  • ACE inhibitors
  • ARBs
  • NSAIDs
  • β-blockers

2. GASTROINTESTINAL EFFECTS

  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhoea
  • Peptic ulcer

3. CNS EFFECTS

  • Drowsiness
  • Mental confusion

4. ANTIANDROGENIC EFFECTS

  • Spironolactone also blocks androgen action.
  • Therefore may cause:
    • Gynaecomastia
    • Decreased libido
    • Menstrual irregularities
    • Impotence

DRUG INTERACTION

ACE INHIBITORS + SPIRONOLACTONE

FLOWCHART: ACE inhibitors -> Decrease aldosterone production FLOWCHART: + Spironolactone -> Blocks aldosterone action -> Marked K+ retention -> Dangerous hyperkalaemia

EPLERENONE

INTRODUCTION

  • Eplerenone is also an aldosterone antagonist.
  • It is more selective for mineralocorticoid receptor.
  • Therefore:
    • Less gynaecomastia
    • Less menstrual irregularities
    • Less impotence

USES

  • Hypertension
  • Chronic heart failure

ADVERSE EFFECTS

  • Hyperkalaemia
  • Gastrointestinal disturbances

DIRECTLY ACTING POTASSIUM-SPARING DIURETICS

AMILORIDE AND TRIAMTERENE

INTRODUCTION

  • These drugs directly block sodium channels in:
    • Late distal tubule
    • Collecting duct
  • They do not block aldosterone receptors.

MECHANISM OF ACTION

  • Normally:
    • Sodium enters tubular cells through sodium channels
    • Potassium is secreted into urine
  • Amiloride and triamterene block this process.
FLOWCHART: Na+ enters tubular cells through Na+ channels -> K+ secretion into urine occurs FLOWCHART: Amiloride/Triamterene block Na+ channels -> Na+ reabsorption decreases -> K+ secretion decreases -> Na+ excretion increases -> K+ retained
  • Amiloride also decreases H+ secretion.
  • Therefore:
    • Acidosis may occur

COMPARISON OF AMILORIDE AND TRIAMTERENE

TABLE: | Feature | Amiloride | Triamterene | | Potency | More potent | Less potent | | Duration | Longer acting | Shorter acting | | Metabolism | Excreted unchanged in urine | Extensively metabolized | | Effect on H+ secretion | Present | Minimal |

THERAPEUTIC USES

1. COMBINATION WITH LOOP OR THIAZIDE DIURETICS

  • Most common use.

WHY COMBINATION IS USEFUL

FLOWCHART: Loop/thiazide diuretics -> Cause hypokalaemia FLOWCHART: Amiloride/Triamterene -> Conserve potassium -> Prevent hypokalaemia
  • Combination therapy also:
    • Improves diuretic effect
    • Improves antihypertensive response

2. LITHIUM-INDUCED NEPHROGENIC DIABETES INSIPIDUS

  • Amiloride is useful.

MECHANISM

FLOWCHART: Lithium enters tubular cells through Na+ channels -> Tubular toxicity develops FLOWCHART: Amiloride blocks Na+ channels -> Lithium entry decreases -> Tubular toxicity decreases

3. CYSTIC FIBROSIS

  • Amiloride aerosol improves:
    • Mucociliary clearance

ADVERSE EFFECTS

MOST IMPORTANT

  • Hyperkalaemia

OTHER ADVERSE EFFECTS

  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhoea
  • Headache
  • Dizziness
  • Muscle cramps

SUMMARY

ALDOSTERONE ANTAGONISTS

  • Spironolactone
  • Eplerenone

MECHANISM

  • Block aldosterone receptor → sodium and water excretion increase while potassium is retained.

DIRECTLY ACTING DRUGS

  • Amiloride
  • Triamterene

MECHANISM

  • Directly block sodium channels in collecting duct → potassium loss decreases.

MOST IMPORTANT ADVERSE EFFECT

  • Hyperkalaemia.
