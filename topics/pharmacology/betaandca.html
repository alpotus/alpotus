BETA-ADRENERGIC BLOCKERS IN ANGINA

Beta blockers are important drugs used mainly in stable exertional angina and unstable angina. Their beneficial effect is mainly due to reduction in heart rate and myocardial contractility.

TABLE: | Commonly Used Beta Blockers in Angina | Examples | | Cardioselective beta blockers | Atenolol, Metoprolol, Bisoprolol | | Nonselective beta blockers | Propranolol, Timolol |
Mnemonic
"Pro Men At Times Beat Angina"
  • Pro = Propranolol
  • Men = Metoprolol
  • At = Atenolol
  • Times = Timolol
  • Beat = Bisoprolol

MECHANISM OF ACTION OF BETA BLOCKERS

The beneficial effect of beta blockers in angina is mainly due to their negative chronotropic and negative inotropic actions.

FLOWCHART: Beta blocker blocks β1 receptors in the heart -> Heart rate decreases -> Myocardial contractility decreases -> The work done by the heart decreases -> Myocardial oxygen consumption decreases -> Anginal pain becomes less frequent FLOWCHART: The heart beats more slowly after beta blocker administration -> Diastole becomes longer -> Coronary blood flow occurs for a longer time -> Oxygen supply to the myocardium improves slightly TABLE: | Cardiac Effect of Beta Blockers | Result | | Negative chronotropic effect | Decreases heart rate | | Negative inotropic effect | Decreases force of contraction | | Reduced cardiac work | Decreases oxygen requirement | | Increased diastolic duration | Improves coronary perfusion |
Mnemonic
"BETA makes the heart Beat Easy To Adapt"
  • B = Blocks β1 receptors
  • E = Exercise tolerance improves
  • T = Tension of myocardium decreases
  • A = Anginal attacks decrease

ROLE OF BETA BLOCKERS IN DIFFERENT TYPES OF ANGINA

Stable (Exertional) Angina

Beta blockers are highly useful in stable angina because they reduce myocardial oxygen demand.

FLOWCHART: Exercise increases heart rate and force of contraction -> Myocardial oxygen demand increases -> Beta blocker prevents the increase in heart rate and contractility -> Myocardial oxygen demand remains lower -> Exertional angina improves

Beta blockers improve exercise tolerance and decrease the frequency of anginal episodes.

They have a slow onset of action and are therefore useful for long-term prophylaxis rather than for immediate relief of acute pain.

TABLE: | Use in Stable Angina | Preferred Drugs | | Long-term prophylaxis | Atenolol, Metoprolol, Bisoprolol |

Cardioselective beta blockers are preferred because they produce fewer pulmonary adverse effects.

After Myocardial Infarction

Beta blockers without intrinsic sympathomimetic activity reduce mortality after myocardial infarction.

TABLE: | Preferred Drugs After MI | Reason | | Atenolol, Metoprolol, Propranolol | Reduce reinfarction and mortality | FLOWCHART: Myocardial infarction damages the myocardium -> The risk of arrhythmia and recurrent infarction increases -> Beta blocker is started early and continued long term -> Heart rate and oxygen demand decrease -> Mortality decreases
Mnemonic
"After MI, Men And Pros Survive"
  • Men = Metoprolol
  • And = Atenolol
  • Pros = Propranolol

Beta blockers with intrinsic sympathomimetic activity such as pindolol should be avoided because they may worsen angina.

Mnemonic
"PINdolol keeps the heart PINching faster"
  • Pindolol has intrinsic sympathomimetic activity
  • Therefore it does not slow the heart sufficiently
  • Hence it should not be used in angina

Unstable Angina

Atenolol and metoprolol are routinely used in unstable angina unless contraindicated.

FLOWCHART: Unstable angina increases sympathetic activity -> Heart rate and contractility rise -> Beta blocker reduces both heart rate and contractility -> Myocardial oxygen demand decreases -> The risk of infarction decreases

Variant (Prinzmetal) Angina

⚠️
Beta blockers are contraindicated in variant angina.
FLOWCHART: Variant angina is caused by coronary artery spasm -> The coronary artery contains both α and β2 receptors -> β2 receptors normally produce vasodilatation -> Beta blocker blocks β2 receptors -> Alpha-mediated vasoconstriction becomes unopposed -> Coronary vasospasm becomes worse -> Variant angina is aggravated TABLE: | Condition | Effect of Beta Blockers | | Stable angina | Beneficial | | Unstable angina | Beneficial | | Variant angina | Contraindicated |
Mnemonic
"Prinzmetal Prefers Pumps Open, Not Blocked"
  • Prinzmetal angina is due to coronary spasm
  • Therefore calcium channel blockers and nitrates are preferred
  • Beta blockers should not be used

ADVERSE EFFECTS OF BETA BLOCKERS

TABLE: | Adverse Effect | Explanation | | Bradycardia | Due to excessive slowing of the SA node | | Heart block | Due to depression of AV conduction | | Bronchospasm | Due to β2 blockade in bronchial smooth muscle | | Worsening of heart failure | Due to excessive negative inotropic effect | | Peripheral vascular insufficiency | May worsen peripheral vascular disease | | Increased left ventricular end-diastolic volume | Due to reduced contractility and slower emptying | FLOWCHART: Beta blocker decreases myocardial contractility -> The left ventricle empties less effectively -> Left ventricular end-diastolic volume increases -> Myocardial wall tension may increase -> This may partially reduce the antianginal benefit FLOWCHART: Beta blocker increases left ventricular end-diastolic volume -> Nitrate is added -> Nitrate decreases venous return and preload -> Left ventricular end-diastolic volume decreases again -> The antianginal effect improves
Mnemonic
"Beta BLOCKS the Body"
  • B = Bradycardia
  • L = Low cardiac output and heart failure
  • O = Obstruction of bronchi causing bronchospasm
  • C = Conduction block in the AV node
  • K = Cold extremities and peripheral vascular disease
  • S = Sudden withdrawal can cause severe angina or MI
⚠️
Beta blockers should never be stopped suddenly.
FLOWCHART: Beta blocker is withdrawn abruptly -> The heart becomes suddenly hypersensitive to catecholamines -> Severe tachycardia and arrhythmia occur -> Angina or myocardial infarction may develop

CALCIUM CHANNEL BLOCKERS (LAQ)

Calcium channel blockers are important antianginal drugs because they reduce oxygen demand and improve coronary blood flow.

TABLE: | Class of Calcium Channel Blocker | Examples | | Phenylalkylamine | Verapamil | | Benzothiazepine | Diltiazem | | Dihydropyridines | Nifedipine, Amlodipine, Cilnidipine, Nicardipine, Felodipine, Isradipine, Nisoldipine, Lacidipine |
Mnemonic
"Very Different Drugs"
  • Very = Verapamil
  • Different = Diltiazem
  • Drugs = Dihydropyridines

MECHANISM OF ACTION OF CALCIUM CHANNEL BLOCKERS

L-type calcium channels are present mainly in cardiac muscle and vascular smooth muscle.

FLOWCHART: Calcium channel blocker binds to the L-type calcium channel -> Entry of calcium into the cell decreases -> Excitation-contraction coupling becomes weaker -> Cardiac contraction decreases and vascular smooth muscle relaxes FLOWCHART: Arterioles dilate after calcium channel blockade -> Peripheral vascular resistance decreases -> Afterload decreases -> The heart performs less work -> Myocardial oxygen demand decreases
Mnemonic
"CCB = Calcium Can't enter the Body"

VERAPAMIL

Verapamil acts mainly on the heart.

FLOWCHART: Verapamil decreases calcium entry into the myocardium and conducting tissue -> Force of contraction decreases -> Heart rate decreases -> AV nodal conduction slows -> Myocardial oxygen demand decreases TABLE: | Effect of Verapamil | Result | | Negative inotropic effect | Decreases force of contraction | | Negative chronotropic effect | Decreases heart rate | | Negative dromotropic effect | Slows AV conduction | | Mild vasodilator action | Slight decrease in afterload |
Mnemonic
"VERAPAMIL Very Effectively Reduces All Pulse Impulses"
  • V = Very strong cardiac depressant
  • E = Effective in supraventricular arrhythmias
  • R = Reduces heart Rate
  • A = AV node conduction decreases

DILTIAZEM

Diltiazem acts on both the heart and blood vessels.

FLOWCHART: Diltiazem decreases calcium entry into cardiac muscle and vascular smooth muscle -> Heart rate and force of contraction decrease moderately -> Coronary and peripheral arteries dilate -> Myocardial oxygen demand decreases TABLE: | Effect of Diltiazem | Result | | Moderate cardiac depression | Decreases heart rate and contractility | | Coronary vasodilatation | Improves coronary blood flow | | Peripheral vasodilatation | Reduces afterload |
Mnemonic
"DILTIAZEM Does It Less Than Verapamil"
  • It is less cardiac depressant than verapamil
  • But more cardiac depressant than dihydropyridines

DIHYDROPYRIDINES

Dihydropyridines act mainly on vascular smooth muscle.

FLOWCHART: Dihydropyridine relaxes vascular smooth muscle -> Coronary and peripheral arteries dilate -> Peripheral vascular resistance decreases -> Afterload decreases -> Myocardial oxygen demand decreases TABLE: | Important Dihydropyridines | Main Feature | | Nifedipine | Prototype drug; rapid vasodilatation and reflex tachycardia | | Amlodipine | Preferred because of long action and fewer side effects | | Nicardipine | More selective for coronary vessels | | Felodipine | More vascular selective | | Nimodipine | Selective for cerebral vessels | | Cilnidipine | Blocks both L-type and N-type channels |
Mnemonic for dihydropyridines
"FINPALC"
  • F = Felodipine
  • I = Isradipine
  • N = Nifedipine
  • P = Nicardipine
  • A = Amlodipine
  • L = Lacidipine
  • C = Cilnidipine

NIFEDIPINE

FLOWCHART: Nifedipine causes rapid vasodilatation -> Blood pressure falls suddenly -> The baroreceptor reflex becomes activated -> Heart rate rises -> Reflex tachycardia and palpitations occur TABLE: | Main Adverse Effects of Nifedipine | | Reflex tachycardia | | Flushing | | Palpitation | | Ankle oedema |

Reflex tachycardia can be reduced by using sustained-release nifedipine or by combining it with a beta blocker.

Mnemonic
"NIFEdipine = Needs Immediate Fast Escape of pulse"
Nifedipine causes the greatest reflex tachycardia among the calcium channel blockers

AMLODIPINE

Amlodipine is the preferred dihydropyridine.

TABLE: | Advantage of Amlodipine | Explanation | | Longer duration of action | Can be given once daily | | Slow absorption | Less reflex tachycardia | | More potent | Better antihypertensive and antianginal effect | | Fewer adverse effects | Less palpitation and flushing |
Mnemonic
"AMLO = A More Lasting Option"

Cilnidipine causes less reflex tachycardia and ankle oedema because it blocks both L-type and N-type calcium channels.

TABLE: | Clinical Condition | Preferred Drugs | | Stable angina | Diltiazem, Verapamil, Amlodipine, Nifedipine SR | | Variant angina | Amlodipine, Nifedipine SR, Diltiazem | | Unstable angina | Used if nitrates or beta blockers are ineffective or contraindicated | | Supraventricular arrhythmia | Verapamil, Diltiazem | | Hypertension | Dihydropyridines, Diltiazem, Verapamil | | Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy | Verapamil | | Migraine prophylaxis | Verapamil, Flunarizine | | Raynaud phenomenon | Nifedipine, Amlodipine, Felodipine, Diltiazem | | Premature labour | Nifedipine | | Cerebral vasospasm after subarachnoid haemorrhage | Nimodipine |
Mnemonic for important special uses
"NIMO for Neurons, NIFE for Newborn, VERA for Veins and Ventricles"
  • Nimodipine → cerebral vasospasm
  • Nifedipine → premature labour
  • Verapamil → arrhythmia and hypertrophic cardiomyopathy

USES OF CALCIUM CHANNEL BLOCKERS (CCBs)

1. Exertional (Stable) Angina

FLOWCHART: CCBs in stable angina -> ↓ Heart rate / ↓ contractility / ↓ afterload -> ↓ Myocardial O₂ consumption -> Coronary artery dilatation -> ↑ Blood flow to myocardium -> Relief of angina
  • The beneficial effect in angina pectoris with CCBs is mainly due to a decrease in myocardial O₂ consumption (following HR, force of contraction or afterload), and dilatation of coronary arteries.
  • DHPs (amlodipine, nifedipine SR and nicardipine), diltiazem and verapamil are used in stable angina.
  • Felodipine, isradipine and nisoldipine are also effective in angina.
  • Diltiazem is preferred to verapamil, as it has fewer side effects.
  • DHPs like nifedipine and felodipine may aggravate anginal symptoms because of reflex tachycardia, which can be counteracted by combining them with β-blockers.

2. Variant Angina

FLOWCHART: Coronary vasospasm -> CCBs relax coronary artery -> Vasospasm decreases -> ↓ Frequency & severity of attacks
  • It is due to coronary spasm.
  • Amlodipine, nifedipine SR and diltiazem are used prophylactically to prevent coronary vasospasm; frequency and severity of attacks is reduced.
  • Combined use of DHPs and nitrates has shown increased efficacy in patients with variant angina.

3. Unstable Angina

FLOWCHART: Symptoms not controlled by nitrates/β-blockers -> CCBs used -> Symptom relief
  • CCBs are used when symptoms are not relieved by nitrates/β-blockers or if these drugs are contraindicated.

4. Supraventricular Arrhythmias

FLOWCHART: Verapamil acts on SA & AV node -> ↓ Conduction velocity -> ↑ Refractory period -> ↓ Ventricular rate -> Control of arrhythmia
  • Verapamil is useful for supraventricular arrhythmias because of its depressant action on SA and AV nodes.
  • It prolongs the refractory period and decreases the conduction velocity of AV node thereby reduces the ventricular rate in atrial flutter or atrial fibrillation.
  • Diltiazem is also useful but is less effective than verapamil.

5. Hypertension

FLOWCHART: CCBs cause vasodilatation -> ↓ Peripheral resistance -> ↓ Blood pressure
  • DHPs, diltiazem and verapamil are used in hypertension.
  • They control BP by their vasodilatory effect.
  • They can be safely used in hypertensive patients with asthma, hyperlipidaemia and renal dysfunction.
  • Nicardipine is used in hypertensive emergencies.

6. Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy

FLOWCHART: Verapamil -> Improves diastolic function -> Symptom relief
  • Verapamil is the preferred CCB, as it improves diastolic function.

7. Migraine Prophylaxis

FLOWCHART: CCBs -> Stabilize vascular tone -> ↓ Frequency of migraine attacks
  • Verapamil is useful for prophylaxis of migraine.
  • Another CCB, flunarizine, is more effective than verapamil in reducing the frequency of migraine attacks.

8. Raynaud's Phenomenon

FLOWCHART: Peripheral vasospasm -> CCBs cause vasodilatation -> Blood flow improves -> Symptoms relieved
  • It is a peripheral vasospastic condition.
  • Nifedipine, amlodipine, felodipine or diltiazem are used to treat this condition.

9. Premature Labour

FLOWCHART: Nifedipine -> Relaxes uterine smooth muscle -> Delays labour
  • Nifedipine is used as uterine relaxant in premature labour.

10. Cerebral Vasospasm

FLOWCHART: Subarachnoid haemorrhage -> Cerebral vasospasm -> Nimodipine -> Prevents vasospasm -> Improves cerebral blood flow
  • Nimodipine is used for prevention and treatment of cerebral vasospasm in patients with subarachnoid haemorrhage.

ADVERSE EFFECTS OF CALCIUM CHANNEL BLOCKERS
