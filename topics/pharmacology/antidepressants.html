ANTIDEPRESSANTS

Depression is a common clinical condition associated with sadness, loss of interest, self-neglect, anorexia, sleep disturbances and, in severe cases, suicidal thoughts.
FLOWCHART: Persistent decrease in mood develops -> The patient loses interest in normal daily activities -> Appetite and sleep become disturbed -> The patient becomes withdrawn and self-neglecting -> In severe cases, suicidal thoughts may occur

PATHOGENESIS OF DEPRESSION

Several hypotheses have been proposed to explain depression.

1. Monoamine Hypothesis

FLOWCHART: The level or function of monoamines in the brain decreases -> Norepinephrine, serotonin and dopamine become deficient in the cortical and limbic system -> Mood, motivation and emotional control become impaired -> Depression develops TABLE: | Neurotransmitter Decreased | Effect | | Serotonin (5-HT) | Sadness, anxiety, sleep disturbance | | Norepinephrine (NE) | Loss of energy and interest | | Dopamine (DA) | Loss of pleasure and motivation |
Mnemonic
"Depression = Less SND"
  • S = Serotonin
  • N = Norepinephrine
  • D = Dopamine

2. Neurotrophic Hypothesis

FLOWCHART: Brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) decreases -> Growth and survival of neurons become impaired -> Neurogenesis decreases -> Depression develops

3. Endocrine Abnormality Hypothesis

FLOWCHART: Abnormality develops in the HPA axis, thyroid hormones or sex steroid levels -> Stress response becomes abnormal -> Mood regulation is disturbed -> Depression develops

TRICYCLIC ANTIDEPRESSANTS (TCAs)

Examples include amitriptyline, imipramine, nortriptyline and clomipramine.

MECHANISM OF ACTION OF TCAs

TCAs increase the amount of norepinephrine and serotonin available in the synaptic cleft.

Effect on Norepinephrine

FLOWCHART: TCA inhibits the norepinephrine transporter (NET) -> Reuptake of norepinephrine into the presynaptic neuron decreases -> The amount of norepinephrine in the synaptic cleft increases -> Norepinephrine acts more strongly on receptors in the CNS -> Antidepressant effect develops

Effect on Serotonin

FLOWCHART: TCA inhibits the serotonin transporter (SERT) -> Reuptake of serotonin into the presynaptic neuron decreases -> The amount of serotonin in the synaptic cleft increases -> Serotonin acts more strongly on receptors in the CNS -> Antidepressant effect develops
Mnemonic
"TCA = Traps Catecholamines and 5-HT outside"

Although monoamine levels increase immediately, clinical improvement appears only after 2–3 weeks.

FLOWCHART: TCA is started -> Norepinephrine and serotonin increase immediately -> Presynaptic inhibitory receptors are stimulated initially -> With continued treatment, these receptors become desensitized -> Negative feedback decreases -> More neurotransmitter is released -> Mood gradually improves after 2–3 weeks FLOWCHART: Monoamine levels in the brain increase -> Brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) increases -> Neurogenesis improves -> Antidepressant effect develops
Mnemonic
"Antidepressants need 2–3 Weeks To Work"

OTHER ACTIONS OF TCAs

TCAs also block muscarinic, α-adrenergic and H1 receptors.

FLOWCHART: TCA -> [Muscarinic Blockade | α-Adrenergic Blockade | H1 Receptor Blockade]

Muscarinic Blockade

FLOWCHART: TCA blocks muscarinic receptors -> Parasympathetic activity decreases -> Dry mouth, blurred vision and constipation occur TABLE: | Muscarinic Blocking Effect | | Dryness of mouth | | Blurring of vision | | Constipation | | Urinary retention | | May precipitate glaucoma |
Mnemonic
"Dry Blind Constipated Bladder"

α-Adrenergic Blockade

FLOWCHART: TCA blocks α-adrenergic receptors -> Peripheral blood vessels dilate -> Postural hypotension develops -> Reflex tachycardia may occur TABLE: | α-Adrenergic Blocking Effects | | Postural hypotension | | Tachycardia | | Cardiac arrhythmias |

H1 Receptor Blockade

FLOWCHART: TCA blocks H1 receptors in the brain -> Sedation and confusion occur TABLE: | H1 Blocking Effects | | Sedation | | Confusion |

TCAs also lower the seizure threshold.

FLOWCHART: TCA is administered in a susceptible patient -> Seizure threshold becomes lower -> Convulsions may occur

PHARMACOKINETICS OF TCAs

TABLE: | Property | Details | | Absorption | Well absorbed from GIT | | Protein binding | Highly bound to plasma proteins | | Distribution | Widely distributed including CNS | | Metabolism | Liver | | Excretion | Urine as inactive metabolites |

Some TCAs produce active metabolites.

TABLE: | Drug | Active Metabolite | | Imipramine | Desipramine | | Amitriptyline | Nortriptyline | FLOWCHART: Imipramine or amitriptyline is administered -> The drug is metabolized in the liver -> An active metabolite is formed -> The duration of action becomes prolonged

ADVERSE EFFECTS OF TCAs

TABLE: | Type of Effect | Manifestation | | Atropine-like effects | Dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation, urinary retention | | α-Blockade | Postural hypotension, tachycardia, arrhythmias | | H1 blockade | Sedation, confusion | | Other effects | Increased appetite, weight gain, convulsions |
Mnemonic
"TCA = Thick, Constipated and Asleep"
  • T = Tachycardia and hypotension
  • C = Constipation and confusion
  • A = Appetite increase and asleep (sedation)

CONTRAINDICATIONS OF TCAs

TABLE: | Contraindication | Reason | | Glaucoma | Anticholinergic action may worsen it | | Epilepsy | Lowers seizure threshold | | Ischaemic heart disease | May precipitate arrhythmias | | Enlarged prostate | Urinary retention may worsen |
Mnemonic
"TCAs should not be given to GEEP"
  • G = Glaucoma
  • E = Epilepsy
  • E = Enlarged prostate
  • P = Problems of the heart (ischaemic heart disease)

MAO INHIBITORS

Monoamine oxidase is a mitochondrial enzyme involved in metabolism of biogenic amines.

TABLE: | Type of MAO | Main Substrate | | MAO-A | Norepinephrine, serotonin and tyramine | | MAO-B | Dopamine |

Moclobemide is a selective and reversible inhibitor of MAO-A.

FLOWCHART: Moclobemide inhibits MAO-A -> Breakdown of norepinephrine and serotonin decreases -> Their level in the brain increases -> Depression improves

Moclobemide has fewer adverse effects.

TABLE: | Advantage of Moclobemide | | Minimal food interactions | | Cheese reaction is rare | | No anticholinergic effects | | No α-blocking effect | | No significant sedation |
Mnemonic
"Moclobemide is a Mild MAO inhibitor"

DRUG INTERACTIONS OF ANTIDEPRESSANTS

1. Interaction of TCAs with MAO Inhibitors

FLOWCHART: TCA and MAO inhibitor are given together -> Monoamine levels increase excessively -> [Hypertensive crisis | Hyperpyrexia | Convulsions] may occur -> Coma may develop TABLE: | Result of TCA + MAO Inhibitor | | Hypertensive crisis | | Hyperpyrexia | | Convulsions | | Coma |

2. Interaction of TCAs with Alcohol and CNS Depressants

FLOWCHART: TCA is given together with alcohol or another CNS depressant -> CNS depressant effect becomes stronger -> Excessive drowsiness and impaired consciousness may occur

3. Serotonin Syndrome

Serotonin syndrome occurs when SSRIs are given together with MAO inhibitors.

SSRI and MAO inhibitor should not be administered together because serotonin syndrome may develop.
FLOWCHART: SSRI and MAO inhibitor are administered together -> Serotonin level in the synapse rises excessively -> The nervous system becomes overactive -> Serotonin syndrome develops TABLE: | Manifestation of Serotonin Syndrome | | Tremor | | Restlessness | | Muscle rigidity | | Hyperthermia | | Sweating | | Shivering | | Seizures | | Coma |
Mnemonic
"Too Much Serotonin makes the patient TRiSH SC"
  • T = Tremor
  • R = Restlessness
  • S = Sweating and shivering
  • H = Hyperthermia
  • S = Seizures
  • C = Coma

4. SSRIs Inhibit Drug Metabolism

FLOWCHART: SSRI is administered together with another drug -> The SSRI inhibits hepatic metabolism of that drug -> The plasma concentration of the second drug rises -> Toxicity may occur TABLE: | Drug Whose Level Increases with SSRIs | | TCAs | | Antipsychotics | | β-blockers | | Phenytoin | | Carbamazepine |

CHEESE REACTION

Normally, tyramine present in food is metabolized in the gut and liver by MAO.

FLOWCHART: Tyramine-containing food is eaten -> MAO in gut and liver normally destroys tyramine -> Very little tyramine reaches the blood
MAO inhibitor blocks destruction of tyramine. Hypertensive crisis and cerebrovascular accident may occur.
FLOWCHART: MAO inhibitor blocks destruction of tyramine -> Tyramine escapes metabolism in the gut and liver -> Large amount of tyramine enters the blood -> Tyramine releases large amounts of norepinephrine from sympathetic nerve endings -> Blood pressure rises suddenly and severely -> Hypertensive crisis and cerebrovascular accident may occur TABLE: | Food Rich in Tyramine | | Cheese | | Fish | | Meat | | Beer | | Wine | | Yeast |

The cheese reaction is treated with intravenous phentolamine.

Mnemonic
"Cheese, Chianti and Crisis"

USES OF ANTIDEPRESSANTS

1. Depression

FLOWCHART: The patient has major depression -> Antidepressant treatment is started -> Interest in daily activities returns -> Mood improves -> Concentration improves and agitation decreases

SSRIs are preferred over TCAs.

TABLE: | Why SSRIs are Preferred | | Better tolerated | | Fewer side effects | | No hypotension | | No significant sedation | | No anticholinergic effects | | Less risk of convulsions | | No cardiac arrhythmias | | Longer duration of action |

2. Anxiety Disorders

SSRIs and SNRIs are useful in anxiety disorders.

FLOWCHART: Anxiety disorder is diagnosed -> SSRI or SNRI is started -> The onset of action is slow -> A benzodiazepine is added temporarily -> Acute anxiety is controlled until the antidepressant begins to act TABLE: | Drugs Useful in Anxiety | | SSRIs | | Venlafaxine | | Duloxetine |

These drugs are also useful in phobias and panic attacks.

3. Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

Mnemonic
"OCD = Clomipramine and Fluvoxamine"

4. ADHD

TABLE: | Drug Useful in ADHD | | Imipramine | | Nortriptyline | | Methylphenidate | | Dextroamphetamine |

5. Nocturnal Enuresis

TABLE: | Drug | Use | | Imipramine | Effective in bedwetting |

6. Migraine Prophylaxis

TABLE: | Drug | Use | | Amitriptyline | Prevents migraine attacks |

7. Chronic Pain and Neuralgias

TABLE: | Drug | Use | | TCAs | Trigeminal neuralgia, herpetic and postherpetic neuralgia | | Venlafaxine and Duloxetine | Fibromyalgia |

8. Atopic Dermatitis

TABLE: | Drug | Use | | Topical doxepin | Antipruritic action |

9. Premature Ejaculation

TABLE: | Drug Useful | | Paroxetine | | Fluoxetine | | Sertraline | | Citalopram | | Dapoxetine |

Dapoxetine acts rapidly.

FLOWCHART: Dapoxetine is taken one hour before intercourse -> Its rapid action delays ejaculation

If SSRIs are not tolerated, clomipramine can be used.

10. Smoking Cessation

Bupropion is useful for smoking cessation.

FLOWCHART: Bupropion inhibits reuptake of norepinephrine and dopamine -> Craving for nicotine decreases -> Smoking becomes easier to stop
Mnemonic
"BUPROPION helps the patient Break the habit"
