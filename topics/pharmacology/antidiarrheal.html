ANTIDIARRHOEAL AGENTS

DEFINITION

Diarrhoea is defined as the passage of unusually loose or watery stools at least 3 times or more within 24 hours (WHO definition).

CLASSIFICATION

TABLE: | Type | Definition | Importance | | Acute diarrhoea | Sudden onset, short duration | Main danger is dehydration | | Chronic diarrhoea | Persists for >30 days | Underlying cause must be identified |
  • In acute diarrhoea, prevention of dehydration is the most important aspect because dehydration is responsible for most deaths.

MANAGEMENT OF DIARRHOEA

  • Oral and parenteral rehydration
  • Antimotility agents
  • Antisecretory agents
  • Probiotics
  • Antimicrobial agents

ORAL REHYDRATION SOLUTION (ORS)

  • In acute diarrhoea, maintenance of water and electrolyte balance is the primary goal.
  • Oral rehydration therapy is:
    • Simplest method
    • Safest method
    • Least expensive method
  • It is the treatment of choice for acute diarrhoea.

COMPOSITION OF WHO LOW-OSMOLARITY ORS

TABLE: | Component | Amount in 1 L water | | Sodium chloride | 2.6 g | | Potassium chloride | 1.5 g | | Sodium citrate | 2.9 g | | Glucose | 13.5 g |

ELECTROLYTE COMPOSITION

TABLE: | Component | Concentration | | Sodium | 75 mM | | Potassium | 20 mM | | Chloride | 65 mM | | Citrate | 10 mM | | Glucose | 75 mM | FLOWCHART: Total osmolarity: 245 mOsm/L -> Low-osmolarity ORS

MECHANISM OF ACTION OF ORS

FLOWCHART: Glucose present in ORS -> Sodium-glucose cotransporter in ileum becomes active -> Sodium absorbed along with glucose -> Water follows passively -> Improved water and electrolyte absorption -> Correction of dehydration

ROLE OF EACH COMPONENT

TABLE: | Component | Function | | Sodium | Replaces sodium loss | | Potassium | Replaces potassium loss | | Glucose | Facilitates sodium absorption | | Citrate | Corrects metabolic acidosis |

ADVANTAGES OF LOW-OSMOLARITY ORS

FLOWCHART: Lower sodium + lower glucose concentration -> Better water absorption -> Reduced stool volume -> Reduced vomiting

OTHER USES OF ORS

  • Cholera
  • Heat stroke
  • Burn patients for maintenance hydration

WHEN IV FLUIDS ARE REQUIRED

FLOWCHART: Severe diarrhoea -> Severe dehydration -> Intravenous fluids indicated

ZINC SUPPLEMENTATION

  • WHO recommends zinc supplementation for 10–14 days in children with acute diarrhoea.

ACTIONS OF ZINC

FLOWCHART: Zinc supplementation -> Decreases intestinal secretion -> Promotes regeneration of intestinal epithelium -> Reduces duration of diarrhoea -> Reduces severity of diarrhoea

ANTIMOTILITY AND ANTISECRETORY AGENTS

OPIOID ANTIMOTILITY AGENTS

CODEINE

  • Natural opium alkaloid
  • Decreases gastrointestinal motility
  • Produces constipation

LIMITATION

FLOWCHART: Codeine -> Abuse potential -> Not preferred for diarrhoea treatment

DIPHENOXYLATE

  • Structurally related to pethidine
  • Reduces GI motility

IMPORTANT FEATURES

FLOWCHART: High doses -> Abuse liability -> Combined with atropine -> Discourages overdose and abuse

ADVERSE EFFECTS

  • Constipation
  • Paralytic ileus
  • Drug addiction

NOTE

  • Banned in many countries

LOPERAMIDE

  • Opiate analogue
  • More potent antidiarrhoeal effect than morphine

MECHANISM OF ACTION

FLOWCHART: Acts on μ-opioid receptors in gut -> Decreases GI motility -> Increases anal sphincter tone -> Reduces frequency of stools

ADDITIONAL ACTION

FLOWCHART: Inhibits secretion induced by -> Cholera toxin -> Some E. coli toxins

PHARMACOLOGICAL FEATURES

TABLE: | Feature | Description | | Oral activity | Effective orally | | Onset | Rapid | | BBB penetration | Poor | | Abuse potential | Absent |

DOSE

FLOWCHART: Initial dose → 4 mg stat -> Then 2 mg after each loose stool -> Maximum dose = 16 mg/day

USES

  • Acute diarrhoea
  • Chronic diarrhoea
  • Traveller’s diarrhoea

ADVERSE EFFECTS

  • Skin rashes
  • Headache
  • Paralytic ileus

CONTRAINDICATION

  • Children younger than 4 years

LIMITATIONS OF ANTIMOTILITY DRUGS

FLOWCHART: Antimotility drugs -> Produce only symptomatic relief -> May retain infective organisms in intestine -> Increased risk of bloodstream invasion
  • Therefore, they should be avoided in:
    • Acute infectious diarrhoea
    • Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD)

CLONIDINE

  • Has both:
    • Antisecretory action
    • Antimotility action

USES

  • Diarrhoea due to opioid withdrawal
  • Diabetic autonomic neuropathy

ADVERSE EFFECTS

  • Depression
  • Hypotension

OCTREOTIDE

  • Octreotide is a somatostatin analogue.

MECHANISM

FLOWCHART: Octreotide -> Inhibits secretion of: 5-HT, VIP, gastrin, insulin -> Reduces intestinal secretion -> Controls secretory diarrhoea

USES

  • Secretory diarrhoea due to GIT and pancreatic tumors
  • AIDS-associated diarrhoea

ROUTE

  • Intravenous
  • Subcutaneous

RACECADOTRIL

  • Racecadotril is a prodrug.
  • Its active metabolite inhibits enkephalinase.

MECHANISM OF ACTION

FLOWCHART: Enkephalinase inhibition -> Decreased degradation of enkephalins -> ↑ Enkephalin concentration in intestinal mucosa -> Reduced intestinal secretion -> Antidiarrhoeal effect

USES

  • Acute secretory diarrhoea
  • Can be used in children

ADVERSE EFFECTS

  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Drowsiness

PROBIOTICS

  • Probiotics contain beneficial microorganisms.

COMMON ORGANISMS

  • Lactobacillus
  • Bifidobacterium
  • Saccharomyces boulardii

MECHANISM OF ACTION

FLOWCHART: Beneficial microorganisms colonize gut -> Compete with pathogens for nutrients and adhesion sites -> Maintain normal gut flora -> Stimulate gut immune response -> Improve intestinal health

USES OF PROBIOTICS

  • Antibiotic-associated diarrhoea
  • Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhoea
  • Ulcerative colitis

IBS

  • Efficacy in IBS remains unclear

SAFETY

  • Generally safe in:
    • Children
    • Adults
    • Elderly

CAUTION

  • Immunocompromised individuals

ANTIMICROBIALS

  • Irrational use of antimicrobials should be avoided.

INDICATIONS

  • Acute bloody diarrhoea
  • Cholera
  • Pseudomembranous enterocolitis
  • Amoebic dysentery

COMMONLY USED ANTIMICROBIALS FOR INFECTIOUS DIARRHOEA

TABLE: | Organism | Drug Regimen | | Shigella spp. | Ciprofloxacin 500 mg BID × 3 days OR Ceftriaxone 2 g IV single dose | | Campylobacter jejuni | Azithromycin 500 mg × 3 days | | Vibrio cholerae | Doxycycline 300 mg single dose OR Azithromycin 1 g single dose | | Clostridium difficile | Metronidazole 800 mg TDS × 10 days | | Entamoeba histolytica | Metronidazole 400 mg TDS + Diloxanide furoate 500 mg TDS × 7 days | | Giardia lamblia | Metronidazole 200 mg TDS × 5 days |

RIFAXIMIN

  • Rifaximin is a poorly absorbed oral antibiotic.

USES

FLOWCHART: Traveller’s diarrhoea caused by E. coli -> IBS with diarrhoea -> Prevention of hepatic encephalopathy
