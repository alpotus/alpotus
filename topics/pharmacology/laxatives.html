LAXATIVES (PURGATIVES, CATHARTICS)

DEFINITION

  • Laxatives facilitate evacuation of formed stools from the bowel.
  • Purgatives cause evacuation of watery stools.
  • The following terms are often used interchangeably:
    • Laxatives
    • Purgatives
    • Aperients
    • Cathartics

CLASSIFICATION (ACCORDING TO MECHANISM OF ACTION)

TABLE: | Class | Drugs | | Bulk-forming laxatives | Bran, methylcellulose, ispaghula (isabgol), psyllium | | Stimulant/Irritant laxatives | Bisacodyl, sodium picosulphate, senna, cascara sagrada, lubiprostone, prucalopride | | Osmotic laxatives | Magnesium sulphate, magnesium hydroxide, sodium phosphate, sodium sulphate, lactulose, lactitol, polyethylene glycol | | Stool softeners (Emollient laxatives) | Docusates, liquid paraffin |

BULK-FORMING LAXATIVES

DRUGS

  • Bran
  • Methylcellulose
  • Psyllium
  • Ispaghula (Isabgol)

MECHANISM OF ACTION

FLOWCHART: Hydrophilic indigestible substances -> Absorb water from intestine -> Swell and increase stool bulk -> Mechanical distension of bowel -> Stimulation of peristalsis -> Evacuation of soft formed stool

IMPORTANT EFFECTS

FLOWCHART: ↑ Water content in stool -> Softening of stool -> Easy defecation

IMPORTANT FEATURES

TABLE: | Feature | Description | | Onset of action | 1–3 days | | Stool type | Formed soft stool | | Main use | Prevention of constipation | | IBS benefit | Psyllium and ispaghula useful | | Additional effect | Pectins bind bile acids and reduce LDL |

ADVERSE EFFECTS

  • Abdominal discomfort
  • Flatulence
  • Intestinal obstruction if inadequate water intake

IMPORTANT POINT

  • Large amount of water should be taken with bulk laxatives.

STOOL SOFTENERS (EMOLLIENT LAXATIVES)

DRUGS

  • Docusates
  • Liquid paraffin

COMPARISON OF STOOL SOFTENERS

TABLE: | Feature | Docusates | Liquid Paraffin | | Nature | Anionic surfactant | Mineral oil | | Mechanism | ↓ Surface tension of stool | Lubricates stool | | Main action | Allows water and fat accumulation in stool | Softens and lubricates stool | | Onset | 1–3 days | Gradual | | Route | Oral/enema | Oral | | Major use | Constipation | Prevent straining in cardiac patients | | Important adverse effect | Increased absorption of liquid paraffin | Lipid pneumonia | | Other adverse effects | — | Vitamin A, D, E, K malabsorption | | Additional issue | — | Leakage causing soiling of clothes |

MECHANISM OF DOCUSATES

FLOWCHART: Anionic surfactant action -> ↓ Surface tension of stool -> Water and fats enter stool -> Softening of feces

MECHANISM OF LIQUID PARAFFIN

FLOWCHART: Mineral oil coats fecal material -> Lubrication of intestinal contents -> Smooth passage of stool

IMPORTANT ADVERSE EFFECTS OF LIQUID PARAFFIN

FLOWCHART: Aspiration into lungs -> Lipid pneumonia FLOWCHART: Long-term use -> ↓ Absorption of fat-soluble vitamins -> Vitamin A, D, E, K deficiency

STIMULANT (IRRITANT) LAXATIVES

DRUGS

  • Bisacodyl
  • Sodium picosulphate
  • Senna
  • Cascara sagrada
  • Lubiprostone
  • Prucalopride

GENERAL MECHANISM

FLOWCHART: Direct stimulation of enteric neurons and intestinal mucosa -> ↑ Prostaglandins and cAMP -> ↓ Na+/K+ ATPase activity -> ↑ Water and electrolyte secretion -> Increased peristalsis -> Evacuation of semifluid stool

COMPARISON OF STIMULANT LAXATIVES

TABLE: | Drug | Important Mechanism | Main Site/Action | Onset | Major Uses | Important Adverse Effects | | Bisacodyl | Local irritant action after activation by intestinal esterases | Colon | Oral: 6–8 h Rectal: 1 h | Constipation, bowel preparation | Irritation, inflammation | | Sodium picosulphate | Stimulant purgative | Colon | Overnight | Colonoscopy/surgery bowel evacuation | Colicky pain | | Senna/Cascara | Converted by bacteria to anthrol | Colon | 6–8 h | Constipation | Melanosis coli, colonic atony | | Lubiprostone | ↑ Intestinal secretion | Intestinal epithelium | Gradual | Chronic constipation, IBS-C | Nausea | | Prucalopride | 5-HT4 agonist → ↑ ACh release | Colon motility | Gradual | Chronic constipation, IBS-C | Headache, diarrhea |

IMPORTANT MECHANISM OF ANTHRAQUINONES (SENNA/CASCARA)

FLOWCHART: Poor absorption in small intestine -> Reach colon -> Bacterial conversion to anthrol -> Local stimulation of colon -> Purgation

MECHANISM OF PRUCALOPRIDE

FLOWCHART: 5-HT4 receptor stimulation -> ↑ Acetylcholine release from enteric neurons -> ↑ Colonic motility -> Improved bowel evacuation

MECHANISM OF LUBIPROSTONE

FLOWCHART: Activation of chloride channels -> ↑ Intestinal fluid secretion -> Softening of stool -> Enhanced bowel movement

IMPORTANT POINTS

TABLE: | Important Point | Explanation | | Chronic use | Can cause atonic colon | | Large doses | Cause fluid and electrolyte loss | | Pregnancy | Contraindicated due to reflex uterine stimulation | | Lactation | Senna/cascara should be avoided |

OSMOTIC LAXATIVES

DRUGS

SALINE OSMOTIC LAXATIVES

  • Magnesium sulphate
  • Magnesium hydroxide
  • Sodium phosphate
  • Sodium sulphate

NON-SALINE OSMOTIC LAXATIVES

  • Lactulose
  • Lactitol
  • Polyethylene glycol

GENERAL MECHANISM

FLOWCHART: Drug is poorly absorbed and remain in intestinal lumen -> Osmotic retention of water -> Bowel distension -> Stimulation of peristalsis -> Evacuation of watery stool

IMPORTANT FEATURES

TABLE: | Feature | Description | | Site of action | Small and large intestine | | Onset | 1–3 hours | | Stool type | Watery stool | | Administration | Early morning with plenty of water | | Additional effect of Mg salts | Release cholecystokinin |

COMPARISON OF OSMOTIC LAXATIVES

TABLE: | Drug/Class | Important Feature | Main Uses | Important Adverse Effect/Contraindication | | Magnesium salts | Saline laxative | Bowel preparation | CNS/cardiac depression in renal failure | | Sodium phosphate | Oral or enema | Bowel evacuation | Avoid in cardiac disease | | Lactulose | Nonabsorbable disaccharide | Constipation, hepatic coma | Flatulence | | Lactitol | Similar to lactulose | Constipation, hepatic encephalopathy | Abdominal discomfort | | Polyethylene glycol | Isosmotic lavage solution | Surgery/endoscopy preparation | Nausea, bloating |

MECHANISM OF LACTULOSE IN HEPATIC COMA

FLOWCHART: Lactulose reaches colon unchanged -> Metabolized by colonic bacteria -> Acidic products formed -> ↓ Colonic pH -> Ammonia converted to ammonium ion -> Ammonium ion not absorbed -> ↓ Blood ammonia level
