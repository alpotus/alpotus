DIURETICS IN CONGESTIVE CARDIAC FAILURE

FLOWCHART: Diuretics -> Promote sodium and water excretion because they act on renal tubules -> Therefore circulating blood volume decreases -> This leads to reduction in preload (ventricular filling pressure) -> Because preload decreases pulmonary congestion reduces -> Peripheral oedema decreases because excess interstitial fluid is mobilized -> Hence symptoms such as dyspnoea and oedema are relieved

Important Points

  • Diuretics provide symptomatic relief but do not affect progression of disease.
  • Furosemide is the commonly used loop diuretic.
  • Torsemide and bumetanide are better absorbed than furosemide.
  • Serum potassium levels should be monitored.
  • Volume contraction should be avoided.
  • In acute pulmonary oedema, furosemide is administered intravenously for rapid relief.
  • Aldosterone antagonists or thiazides can be added to loop diuretics to overcome diuretic resistance.

ALDOSTERONE ANTAGONISTS

Pathological Effect of Aldosterone

FLOWCHART: Increased aldosterone in CHF -> Sodium and water retention increases -> Circulating volume increases -> Preload increases -> Congestion worsens

Mechanism of Aldosterone Antagonists

FLOWCHART: Aldosterone antagonists (spironolactone/eplerenone) -> Block aldosterone receptors -> Decrease sodium and water retention -> Reduce preload -> Decrease congestion -> Prevent ventricular remodeling -> Slow disease progression -> Decrease mortality

Important Points

  • Aldosterone increases potassium and magnesium loss → hypokalaemia, hypomagnesaemia.
  • This increases risk of arrhythmias.
  • Aldosterone also causes ventricular hypertrophy and remodeling.
  • These drugs are used in combination with other drugs in moderate to severe heart failure.

VASODILATORS

Classification (Based on Action)

  • Mixed arteriolar and venodilators: ACE inhibitors, ARBs, sodium nitroprusside
  • Venodilators: Nitrates
  • Arteriolar dilators: Hydralazine, minoxidil, nicorandil

MECHANISM – GENERAL

FLOWCHART: Vasodilators -> Dilate blood vessels -> Venodilation reduces venous return -> Preload decreases -> Arteriolar dilation reduces peripheral resistance -> Afterload decreases -> Stroke volume increases -> Cardiac output improves -> Tissue perfusion improves

ACE INHIBITORS

Hemodynamic Effect

FLOWCHART: ACE inhibitors -> Inhibit conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II -> Decrease angiotensin II levels -> Peripheral vasodilation occurs -> Afterload decreases -> Stroke volume increases -> Tissue perfusion improves

Effect on Preload

FLOWCHART: Decreased angiotensin II -> Decreased aldosterone secretion -> Sodium and water retention decreases -> Circulating volume decreases -> Preload decreases -> Congestion reduces

Effect on Remodeling and Mortality

FLOWCHART: Decreased angiotensin II and aldosterone -> Ventricular remodeling decreases -> Hypertrophy reduces -> Disease progression slows -> Mortality decreases

Important Points

  • Standard therapy for all grades of CHF.
  • Also used in asymptomatic LV systolic dysfunction.
  • Increase renal blood flow → diuresis.
  • Decrease atrial and pulmonary pressures → reduce congestion.
  • Improve symptoms, reduce hospitalization and mortality.

ANGIOTENSIN RECEPTOR BLOCKERS (ARBs)

FLOWCHART: ARBs -> Block AT1 receptors -> Prevent effects of angiotensin II -> Produce effects similar to ACE inhibitors -> Reduce preload and afterload -> Improve cardiac output

Important Points

  • Used in patients intolerant to ACE inhibitors.
  • Avoid ACE inhibitor side effects like cough, angioedema.

DIRECT RENIN INHIBITOR

FLOWCHART: Aliskiren -> Inhibits renin -> Decreases angiotensin I and II -> Decreases blood pressure -> Reduces LV mass -> Produces beneficial effects in heart failure

NEPRILYSIN INHIBITOR (SACUBITRIL)

FLOWCHART: Sacubitril -> Inhibits neprilysin -> Decreases breakdown of ANP and BNP -> Natriuresis and diuresis increase -> Vasodilation occurs -> Cardiac load decreases -> Symptoms improve

Important Point

  • Used with valsartan in severe heart failure.

OTHER VASODILATORS

Sodium Nitroprusside & Nitroglycerin

  • Used intravenously in severe heart failure.

Hydralazine

FLOWCHART: Hydralazine -> Arteriolar dilation -> Afterload decreases -> Cardiac output increases

Important Points

  • Reflex tachycardia can occur.
  • Fluid retention may occur.
  • Tachycardia is less with mixed vasodilators.

β-ADRENERGIC BLOCKERS

FLOWCHART: Beta blockers -> Block beta receptors -> Decrease effects of catecholamines -> Decrease heart rate and myocardial oxygen demand -> Improve LV structure and function -> Decrease ventricular remodeling -> Increase ejection fraction -> Slow disease progression -> Reduce mortality

Important Points

  • Used in mild to moderate heart failure.
  • Improve symptoms and reduce hospitalization.
  • Decrease arrhythmias.
  • Carvedilol has antioxidant effect.
  • Should not be used in decompensated heart failure.

SYMPATHOMIMETIC AMINES

DOPAMINE (LOW DOSE)

FLOWCHART: Dopamine (low dose <2 mcg/kg/min) -> Acts on D receptors -> Renal vasodilation -> Increased GFR -> Increased urine output

DOPAMINE (MODERATE DOSE)

FLOWCHART: Dopamine (moderate dose 2–5 mcg/kg/min) -> Stimulates beta receptors -> Increases myocardial contractility -> Cardiac output increases

DOPAMINE (HIGH DOSE)

FLOWCHART: Dopamine (high dose >10 mcg/kg/min) -> Alpha receptor stimulation -> Vasoconstriction -> Afterload increases -> Renal blood flow decreases -> Beneficial effects lost

Important Points

  • Used in acute heart failure with renal impairment.

DOBUTAMINE

FLOWCHART: Dobutamine -> Stimulates beta-1 receptors -> Increases myocardial contractility -> Cardiac output increases -> Improves tissue perfusion

Important Points

  • Minimal effect on heart rate and BP.
  • Used in acute heart failure (post-MI, surgery).
  • Side effects: tachycardia, hypertension, tolerance.

PHOSPHODIESTERASE-3 INHIBITORS

Mechanism

FLOWCHART: PDE-3 inhibitors -> Inhibit phosphodiesterase-3 -> Increase cAMP -> Increase intracellular calcium -> Positive inotropic effect -> Vasodilation occurs -> Cardiac output increases -> Afterload decreases

Levosimendan

FLOWCHART: Levosimendan -> Opens potassium channels -> Causes vasodilation -> Increases sensitivity of troponin C to calcium -> Enhances myocardial contraction

Important Points

  • Used in severe heart failure (short-term IV).
  • Inamrinone → thrombocytopenia, hepatotoxicity.
  • Milrinone → more potent, less toxicity.
  • Levosimendan → hypotension.

VASOPRESSIN RECEPTOR ANTAGONISTS

FLOWCHART: Tolvaptan/Conivaptan -> Block vasopressin receptors -> Free water excretion increases -> Serum sodium increases -> Volume overload reduces -> Symptoms improve
  • Useful in CHF with hyponatraemia.

NATRIURETIC PEPTIDE

FLOWCHART: Nesiritide (BNP) -> Vasodilation -> Decreases preload and afterload -> Improves cardiac output -> Reduces dyspnoea
  • Hypotension is common adverse effect.

MANAGEMENT OF CCF

General Strategy

  • Diet: Salt restriction
  • Diuretics: Reduce congestion
  • Vasodilators: Reduce preload & afterload
  • Digoxin: Improve contractility
  • Dopamine/Dobutamine: Acute support
  • PDE-3 inhibitors: Severe HF
  • β-blockers: Long-term mortality benefit

Key Points

  • ACE inhibitors, ARBs, aldosterone antagonists, sacubitril, β-blockers → slow disease progression
  • Diuretics, vasodilators, inotropes → symptomatic relief
