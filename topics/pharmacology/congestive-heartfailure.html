DRUGS USED IN CONGESTIVE CARDIAC FAILURE (CCF)

  • Congestive cardiac failure is a condition in which the heart fails to pump an adequate amount of blood to the tissues because myocardial contraction becomes inefficient, which leads to a reduction in cardiac output.

COMPENSATORY MECHANISMS IN CCF

FLOWCHART: Reduced cardiac output in CCF -> Because tissues are receiving inadequate perfusion -> Body activates compensatory mechanisms to maintain circulation -> Increased sympathetic activity occurs because of baroreceptor stimulation -> Therefore heart rate and contractility initially increase -> Simultaneously renin–angiotensin–aldosterone system is activated because of reduced renal perfusion -> This leads to sodium and water retention and vasoconstriction -> In addition, myocardial hypertrophy and remodeling occur because of chronic pressure and volume overload -> These mechanisms initially help to maintain cardiac output -> However, with time they become maladaptive because they increase workload on heart -> Therefore compensatory mechanisms fail -> Clinical features of heart failure appear progressively

HEMODYNAMIC DISTURBANCES

Backward Failure

FLOWCHART: Backward failure occurs because blood accumulates behind the failing ventricle -> This leads to increased pulmonary capillary pressure -> Therefore pulmonary congestion develops -> Resulting in dyspnoea and orthopnoea

Forward Failure

FLOWCHART: Forward failure occurs because cardiac output is reduced -> Therefore peripheral tissues receive inadequate oxygen -> This leads to tissue hypoxia -> Resulting in fatigue and generalized weakness

THERAPEUTIC GOALS

FLOWCHART: Goals of treatment in CCF -> Because the primary problem is reduced cardiac output and congestion -> Treatment aims to relieve symptoms and improve survival -> Therefore preload is reduced because excess fluid worsens congestion -> Afterload is reduced because high resistance increases cardiac workload -> Contractility is enhanced because stronger contraction improves cardiac output -> Thus symptoms improve and disease progression slows and mortality decreases

MECHANISM OF ACTION OF DIGOXIN

FLOWCHART: Digoxin mechanism -> Digoxin binds to and inhibits Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase enzyme present on myocardial cell membrane because this enzyme normally pumps Na⁺ out and K⁺ into the cell -> Due to inhibition of this pump intracellular sodium concentration increases -> Because of increased intracellular Na⁺ the Na⁺/Ca²⁺ exchanger activity decreases -> Therefore less calcium is extruded from the cell -> This leads to accumulation of intracellular calcium -> Additionally calcium entry through L-type calcium channels during action potential increases -> Increased calcium is stored in sarcoplasmic reticulum -> During each action potential more calcium is released -> Therefore actin–myosin interaction increases -> This results in increased force of myocardial contraction (positive inotropic effect) -> Hence cardiac output increases

PHARMACOLOGICAL ACTIONS

1. EFFECT ON CONTRACTILITY

FLOWCHART: Positive inotropic effect -> Because intracellular calcium increases -> Force of myocardial contraction increases -> Ventricles empty more completely during systole -> Therefore end-diastolic volume decreases -> This reduces ventricular size -> Because muscle fibre length decreases oxygen requirement of myocardium decreases -> Therefore heart performs more work with same energy -> Hence cardiac output improves and congestion reduces

2. EFFECT ON HEART RATE

FLOWCHART: Effect on heart rate -> Digoxin stimulates vagus nerve because of central vagomimetic action -> This increases parasympathetic tone on SA node -> Therefore rate of impulse generation decreases -> Hence heart rate decreases (negative chronotropic effect)

TOXIC DOSE EFFECT ON HEART RATE

FLOWCHART: However in toxic doses -> Sympathetic activity increases due to direct and indirect effects -> Therefore heart rate may increase and arrhythmias may occur

3. EFFECT ON AV NODE

FLOWCHART: Effect on AV node -> Digoxin increases vagal tone -> This prolongs effective refractory period (ERP) of AV node -> Conduction velocity decreases -> Therefore fewer impulses are transmitted from atria to ventricles -> Hence ventricular rate is controlled in atrial fibrillation and flutter

TOXIC DOSE EFFECT ON AV NODE

FLOWCHART: At higher concentrations -> Increased automaticity occurs due to direct action -> Leading to atrial and ventricular arrhythmias

ECG CHANGES

TABLE: | Feature | Change | | PR interval | Prolonged because AV conduction slows | | ST segment | Depression due to altered repolarization | | T wave | Inversion |

EXTRACARDIAC ACTIONS

FLOWCHART: Renal effect -> Because cardiac output improves -> Renal blood flow increases -> Glomerular filtration rate increases -> Sodium and water excretion increases -> Therefore edema decreases and congestion is relieved

ADVERSE EFFECTS

TABLE: | System | Effects | | GIT | Anorexia, nausea, vomiting | | CNS | Confusion, visual disturbances, disorientation | | Cardiac | Ventricular arrhythmias, AV block, tachycardia | | Others | Skin rash, gynaecomastia |

MECHANISM OF MAJOR ADVERSE EFFECTS

1. ARRHYTHMIAS

FLOWCHART: Arrhythmias due to digoxin -> Excess digoxin inhibits Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase excessively -> Intracellular calcium becomes very high -> This causes delayed after-depolarizations -> Because of increased automaticity ectopic pacemaker activity develops -> Therefore abnormal impulses are generated -> Leading to atrial and ventricular arrhythmias

2. AV BLOCK / BRADYCARDIA

FLOWCHART: AV block -> Digoxin increases vagal tone significantly -> AV nodal conduction is markedly slowed -> Effective refractory period increases -> Therefore impulse conduction is blocked -> Leading to bradycardia and AV block

3. GIT EFFECTS

FLOWCHART: GIT toxicity -> Digoxin stimulates chemoreceptor trigger zone (CTZ) in brain -> Also causes direct irritation of gastrointestinal mucosa -> Therefore nausea and vomiting occur -> Which are often early signs of toxicity

4. VISUAL DISTURBANCE

FLOWCHART: Visual disturbance -> Digoxin affects retinal and CNS function -> Alters perception of color due to neuronal toxicity -> Therefore patients perceive yellow or green halos (xanthopsia)

FACTORS AFFECTING TOXICITY

TABLE: | Factor | Explanation | | Hypokalaemia | Increases binding of digoxin to Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase → ↑ toxicity | | Hypercalcaemia | Enhances arrhythmogenic potential | | Hypomagnesaemia | Increases sensitivity | | Elderly | Reduced renal clearance | | Renal failure | Drug accumulates | | Thyroid disorders | Alter metabolism and sensitivity |

TREATMENT OF DIGOXIN TOXICITY

  • Digoxin should be stopped immediately because continued exposure worsens toxicity
  • Electrolyte imbalance should be corrected because potassium levels influence toxicity
  • Arrhythmias should be treated specifically depending on type
  • Antidote should be given in severe toxicity
FLOWCHART: Treatment -> Digoxin toxicity diagnosed -> Drug discontinued immediately -> Serum potassium assessed -> If potassium is low -> Potassium chloride administered because it reduces digoxin binding -> If arrhythmias occur -> Supraventricular arrhythmias treated with propranolol -> Ventricular arrhythmias treated with lignocaine because it is safe and short acting -> If bradycardia or AV block occurs -> Atropine or cardiac pacing used -> In severe life-threatening toxicity -> Digoxin-specific antibodies (Digibind) administered -> Which bind circulating digoxin and neutralize its effects

DRUG INTERACTIONS

  • Drug interactions occur because digoxin affects conduction, electrolyte balance and pharmacokinetics.

Cholestyramine Interaction

FLOWCHART: Cholestyramine binds digoxin in gut -> Therefore absorption decreases

Diuretics Interaction

FLOWCHART: Loop/thiazide diuretics cause hypokalaemia -> Therefore digoxin toxicity increases

β-blockers and Verapamil Interaction

FLOWCHART: β-blockers and verapamil depress AV node -> Therefore combined effect causes severe AV block

Calcium Interaction

FLOWCHART: Calcium increases intracellular calcium -> Therefore arrhythmias increase

Sympathomimetics Interaction

FLOWCHART: Sympathomimetics increase automaticity -> Therefore risk of arrhythmias increases

USES OF DIGITALIS

1. CONGESTIVE CARDIAC FAILURE

  • Digitalis is useful in low-output heart failure because it increases myocardial contractility and improves circulation.
FLOWCHART: CCF -> Digoxin increases intracellular calcium -> Therefore myocardial contractility increases -> Cardiac output increases -> Renal perfusion improves -> Sodium and water excretion increases -> Therefore edema and pulmonary congestion decrease -> Resulting in relief of dyspnoea and improved tissue perfusion

2. ATRIAL FIBRILLATION

  • Digitalis reduces ventricular rate because it slows AV nodal conduction through vagal stimulation.
FLOWCHART: Atrial fibrillation -> Digoxin increases vagal tone -> AV nodal conduction slows -> Fewer impulses reach ventricles -> Ventricular rate decreases -> Therefore cardiac efficiency improves

3. ATRIAL FLUTTER

  • Digitalis controls ventricular response by prolonging AV nodal refractory period.
FLOWCHART: Atrial flutter -> Digoxin increases AV node refractory period -> Impulse conduction decreases -> Ventricular rate reduces -> Therefore symptoms improve

4. PSVT

  • Digitalis terminates PSVT by increasing vagal tone, although it is not preferred in acute management.
FLOWCHART: PSVT -> Digoxin increases parasympathetic activity -> SA and AV node activity decreases -> Abnormal re-entry circuits are suppressed -> Therefore arrhythmia terminates
