Thiazide Diuretics

Thiazides and related drugs are widely used in uncomplicated hypertension. The commonly used drugs are chlorothiazide, hydrochlorothiazide and chlorthalidone.

These drugs have a long duration of action and are usually administered once daily.

TABLE: | Drug | Common Antihypertensive Dose | | Hydrochlorothiazide | 12.5–25 mg/day | | Chlorthalidone | 12.5–25 mg/day |

The preferred starting dose is low because low doses provide adequate antihypertensive effect with fewer adverse effects.

FLOWCHART: Thiazide therapy is started with 12.5 mg of hydrochlorothiazide or chlorthalidone -> The patient is treated for 2 to 4 weeks -> The antihypertensive response is assessed -> If blood pressure remains high, the dose may be increased to 25 mg/day -> Doses higher than 25 mg/day increase adverse effects without significant additional benefit

The antihypertensive effect of thiazides develops gradually over 2–4 weeks.

Mechanism of Action of Thiazides

Thiazides act in the early part of the distal convoluted tubule.

TABLE: | Site of Action | Transporter Blocked | | Early distal convoluted tubule | Sodium-chloride symporter | FLOWCHART: Thiazide reaches the early distal convoluted tubule of the nephron -> The sodium-chloride symporter is inhibited -> Sodium and chloride reabsorption decrease -> More sodium and water are excreted in urine -> Blood volume falls -> Blood pressure decreases

Initially, thiazides lower blood pressure by reducing plasma volume. During chronic therapy, an additional effect develops in blood vessels.

FLOWCHART: Thiazide is continued for several weeks -> The sodium concentration inside vascular smooth muscle decreases -> The vascular smooth muscle becomes less responsive to vasoconstrictors -> Peripheral vascular resistance decreases -> Blood pressure falls further

Thus, chronic antihypertensive action is mainly due to reduction in peripheral vascular resistance.

Adverse Effects of Thiazides

The adverse effects of thiazides are mainly due to loss of potassium and metabolic changes.

TABLE: | Adverse Effect | Explanation | | Hypokalaemia | Increased potassium loss in urine | | Hyperglycaemia | Potassium loss decreases insulin release | | Hyperuricaemia | Uric acid excretion decreases | | Hyperlipidaemia | Serum cholesterol and triglycerides may increase | | Hypercalcaemia | Urinary calcium excretion decreases | | Impotence and decreased libido | May occur during long-term therapy |

Hypokalaemia is one of the most important adverse effects.

FLOWCHART: Thiazide increases sodium delivery to the collecting tubule -> More sodium enters the collecting tubule cells -> Potassium secretion into urine increases -> Serum potassium level falls -> Hypokalaemia develops

Hyperglycaemia occurs because potassium depletion interferes with insulin release.

FLOWCHART: Thiazide causes hypokalaemia -> Low potassium decreases insulin secretion from pancreatic beta cells -> Blood glucose level rises -> Hyperglycaemia develops

Hyperuricaemia occurs because thiazides reduce uric acid excretion.

FLOWCHART: Thiazide competes with uric acid for renal excretion -> Less uric acid is excreted in urine -> Serum uric acid rises -> Hyperuricaemia and gout may occur

Advantages of Thiazides as Antihypertensives

Thiazides have several advantages that make them very useful in uncomplicated hypertension.

TABLE: | Advantage | Reason | | Long duration of action | Can be administered once daily | | Low cost | Inexpensive compared with many other antihypertensives | | Good tolerance in elderly patients | Particularly useful in older individuals | | Reduced fracture risk in elderly | They decrease urinary calcium loss | | Useful in combination therapy | Produce synergistic effect with other antihypertensives |

The reduction in urinary calcium excretion is beneficial in elderly patients.

FLOWCHART: Thiazide decreases calcium excretion in urine -> More calcium is retained in the body -> Bone mineral density improves -> Risk of fracture decreases in elderly patients

Certain thiazide-like drugs have special advantages.

TABLE: | Drug | Special Feature | | Chlorthalidone | Frequently used because of very long duration of action | | Indapamide | More potent and longer acting with fewer adverse effects | | Metolazone | More potent and longer acting with fewer adverse effects |

Loop Diuretics

Loop diuretics have a short duration of action. Therefore, they do not maintain a sustained sodium deficit and are not routinely used in uncomplicated hypertension.

TABLE: | Loop Diuretic | Common Drugs | | Examples | Furosemide, Bumetanide, Torsemide | FLOWCHART: Loop diuretic produces marked sodium and water loss -> The effect lasts only for a short period -> Sodium retention returns between doses -> A sustained fall in blood pressure is not maintained -> Loop diuretics are not routinely used in uncomplicated hypertension

Loop diuretics are mainly used when hypertension is associated with renal failure or cardiac failure.

TABLE: | Clinical Situation | Reason for Use | | Hypertension with renal failure | More effective than thiazides when kidney function is poor | | Hypertension with heart failure | Remove excess fluid rapidly |

Potassium-Sparing Diuretics

Potassium-sparing diuretics are usually used together with thiazides or loop diuretics.

TABLE: | Drug Group | Examples | | Aldosterone antagonists | Spironolactone, Eplerenone | | Sodium channel blockers | Amiloride, Triamterene |

Their main purpose is to increase antihypertensive efficacy and prevent hypokalaemia.

FLOWCHART: Thiazide or loop diuretic causes potassium loss -> Potassium-sparing diuretic is added -> Potassium excretion decreases -> Serum potassium remains normal -> The antihypertensive effect becomes stronger and safer

Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs)

Calcium channel blockers are among the first-line drugs used in hypertension.

TABLE: | Major Types of Calcium Channel Blockers | Examples | | Dihydropyridines (DHPs) | Nifedipine, Amlodipine, Cilnidipine, Felodipine, Nicardipine, Isradipine | | Non-Dihydropyridines | Verapamil, Diltiazem |

The main antihypertensive effect of calcium channel blockers is due to relaxation of vascular smooth muscle.

FLOWCHART: Calcium normally enters vascular smooth muscle through calcium channels -> Calcium channel blocker prevents calcium entry -> The smooth muscle of arterioles relaxes -> Peripheral vasodilatation occurs -> Peripheral vascular resistance decreases -> Blood pressure falls TABLE: | Important Feature of CCBs | Explanation | | No electrolyte disturbance | Unlike diuretics, they do not alter sodium or potassium significantly | | No hyperuricaemia | They do not increase uric acid levels | | No change in lipid profile | They do not produce hyperlipidaemia | | No sexual dysfunction | They are less likely to cause impotence or decreased libido |

Calcium channel blockers are especially preferred in patients older than 55 years.

TABLE: | Clinical Situation | Preferred Drug | | Hypertension in elderly patients (>55 years) | Calcium channel blockers | | Hypertension during pregnancy | Nifedipine | | Hypertension with diabetes | Dihydropyridines | | Hypertension with asthma | Dihydropyridines | | Hypertension with angina | Dihydropyridines | | Hypertensive emergency | Intravenous nicardipine |

Dihydropyridines are useful in diabetic patients because they delay progression of renal complications and microalbuminuria.

FLOWCHART: Hypertension and diabetes increase intraglomerular pressure -> Renal damage gradually progresses -> Dihydropyridine calcium channel blocker lowers blood pressure -> Microalbuminuria decreases -> Progression of diabetic nephropathy slows

Adverse Effects of Calcium Channel Blockers

The adverse effects differ between dihydropyridines and non-dihydropyridines.

Adverse Effects of Dihydropyridines

TABLE: | Drug Group | Common Adverse Effects | | Dihydropyridines | Headache, flushing, ankle oedema, palpitation, reflex tachycardia |

Reflex tachycardia is especially important with nifedipine.

FLOWCHART: Nifedipine produces rapid vasodilatation -> Blood pressure falls suddenly -> The baroreceptor reflex becomes activated -> Sympathetic activity increases -> Heart rate rises -> Reflex tachycardia develops

Reflex tachycardia due to nifedipine can be prevented.

FLOWCHART: Sustained-release nifedipine is used or a beta blocker is added -> The fall in blood pressure becomes gradual -> Baroreceptor stimulation decreases -> Reflex tachycardia is prevented

Adverse Effects of Verapamil and Diltiazem

Verapamil and diltiazem have greater depressant action on the heart.

TABLE: | Drug | Important Problem | | Verapamil | Cardiac depression, bradycardia and worsening of heart failure | | Diltiazem | Cardiac depression and conduction defects | FLOWCHART: Verapamil or diltiazem blocks calcium entry into cardiac muscle and conducting tissue -> Cardiac contractility decreases -> Heart rate and conduction slow down -> Cardiac dysfunction may worsen
⚠️
Therefore verapamil and diltiazem should be avoided in patients with pre-existing cardiac dysfunction or heart failure.
