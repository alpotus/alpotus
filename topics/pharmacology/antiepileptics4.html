VALPROIC ACID (SODIUM VALPROATE)

Sodium valproate is a broad-spectrum antiepileptic drug.

MECHANISM OF ACTION OF VALPROATE

Valproate acts through three different mechanisms.

Valproate Mechanism │ ├── Sodium Channel Blockade │ └── Valproate delays recovery of sodium channels from inactivation │ └── Neuronal excitability decreases │ ├── T-Type Calcium Channel Blockade │ └── Valproate blocks T-type calcium current in thalamic neurons │ └── Absence seizures are suppressed │ └── Increase in GABA Activity ├── Valproate stimulates glutamic acid decarboxylase (GAD) │ └── Synthesis of GABA increases └── Valproate inhibits GABA-transaminase (GABA-T) └── Breakdown of GABA decreases → GABA level in brain increases → Neuronal excitability decreases
Mnemonic
"VALPROATE works by 3 S"
  • S = Sodium channel block
  • S = Stops T-type calcium current
  • S = Strengthens GABA

USES OF VALPROATE

TABLE: | Use | | Absence seizures | | Myoclonic seizures | | Partial seizures | | Generalized tonic-clonic seizures | | Mania | | Bipolar disorder | | Migraine prophylaxis |

Because valproate is effective in many different seizure types, it is called a broad-spectrum antiepileptic drug.

DRUG INTERACTIONS

Metabolic Inhibition

FLOWCHART: Sodium valproate -> Inhibits metabolism of phenobarbitone and lamotrigine -> ↑ plasma levels of these drugs -> ↑ risk of toxicity

Phenytoin Interaction

FLOWCHART: Sodium valproate -> Displaces phenytoin from plasma protein binding sites -> Also decreases its metabolism -> ↑ free phenytoin levels -> ↑ toxicity risk

Carbamazepine Interaction

FLOWCHART: Sodium valproate -> Inhibits hydrolysis of active epoxide metabolite of carbamazepine -> ↑ accumulation of active metabolite -> ↑ toxicity

Clonazepam Interaction

FLOWCHART: Sodium valproate + clonazepam -> May precipitate absence status
  • Sodium valproate is not an enzyme inducer, therefore it does not reduce the efficacy of steroidal contraceptives.

DIVALPROEX

Divalproex contains valproic acid and sodium valproate in a 1:1 ratio.

STATUS EPILEPTICUS

⚠️ Status epilepticus is a medical emergency.
FLOWCHART: Repeated tonic-clonic seizures occur -> The patient does not regain consciousness between attacks -> [Or a single seizure continues for more than 5 minutes] -> Status epilepticus develops

TREATMENT OF STATUS EPILEPTICUS

Initial management must be done immediately.

FLOWCHART: Initial Management -> [Hospitalize the patient | Maintain airway and establish intravenous line | Administer oxygen | Collect blood for glucose, calcium, electrolytes and urea estimation | Maintain fluid and electrolyte balance]

Step 1

FLOWCHART: Step 1 -> [Lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg intravenous slowly at a maximum rate of 2 mg/minute | Diazepam 10 mg intravenous slowly] -> [Repeat after 10 minutes if necessary | Watch carefully for hypotension and respiratory depression] FLOWCHART: Step 1 Continued -> [Fosphenytoin 20 mg PE/kg intravenous in normal saline or glucose | Phenytoin 20 mg/kg intravenous infusion at 50 mg/minute in normal saline] -> [Monitor cardiac rhythm and blood pressure]

Step 2

FLOWCHART: Seizure continues despite Step 1 -> Phenobarbitone 10–15 mg/kg intravenous infusion at 100 mg/minute is given -> Watch for respiratory depression

Step 3

FLOWCHART: Seizure still continues -> General anaesthesia is induced with intravenous midazolam or propofol -> Airway and blood pressure are maintained
Mnemonic
"LPP"
  • L = Lorazepam
  • P = Phenytoin or Fosphenytoin
  • P = Phenobarbitone
FLOWCHART: If this fails -> [Propofol | Midazolam] under general anaesthesia
