MUCOLYTICS

Mucolytics are drugs that break thick, sticky, tenacious sputum and reduce its viscosity so that mucus can be expelled more easily with less effort during coughing.

MAIN EFFECTS OF MUCOLYTICS

  • Liquefy thick sputum
  • Improve sputum clearance
  • Make cough more productive
  • Reduce fatigue caused by excessive coughing

CLASSIFICATION

BROMHEXINE

  • Semisynthetic mucolytic
  • Oral administration

ACETYLCYSTEINE

  • Aerosol/inhalational mucolytic

CARBOCISTEINE

  • Oral mucolytic

BROMHEXINE

INTRODUCTION

  • Bromhexine is a semisynthetic mucolytic drug with both:
    • Mucolytic action
    • Mucokinetic action

MUCOkinetic ACTION MEANS

  • Increased volume of bronchial secretions
  • Decreased sputum viscosity
  • Easier expectoration

MECHANISM OF ACTION

FLOWCHART: Bromhexine -> Liberates lysosomal enzymes -> Lysosomal enzymes digest mucopolysaccharides in sputum -> Viscosity of sputum decreases -> Sputum becomes thinner -> Cough becomes productive and less tiring

PHARMACOLOGICAL EFFECTS

  • Decreases thickness of sputum
  • Facilitates expectoration
  • Improves clearance of bronchial secretions
  • Makes cough less exhausting

ROUTE OF ADMINISTRATION

  • Oral

ADVERSE EFFECTS

  • Rhinorrhoea
  • Lacrimation

ACETYLCYSTEINE

INTRODUCTION

  • Acetylcysteine is a mucolytic commonly used as:
    • Aerosol/inhalational therapy
  • It is useful in conditions associated with thick mucus secretions.

MECHANISM OF ACTION

FLOWCHART: Acetylcysteine -> Breaks disulphide bonds in mucoproteins of sputum -> Sputum becomes thin and less viscid -> Expectoration becomes easier -> Cough becomes productive and less tiring

PHARMACOLOGICAL EFFECTS

  • Liquefies thick mucus
  • Reduces sputum viscosity
  • Improves sputum clearance

ROUTE OF ADMINISTRATION

  • Aerosol/Inhalation

ADVERSE EFFECTS

  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Bronchospasm

CARBOCISTEINE

INTRODUCTION

  • Carbocisteine is an orally active mucolytic drug.

MECHANISM OF ACTION

FLOWCHART: Carbocisteine -> Breaks disulphide bonds in mucoproteins -> Decreases viscosity of sputum -> Facilitates sputum expulsion

ROUTE OF ADMINISTRATION

  • Oral

ADVERSE EFFECTS

  • Gastric irritation
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting

CONTRAINDICATION / PRECAUTION

  • Carbocisteine should be avoided in:
    • Peptic ulcer disease
  • because it may aggravate gastric irritation.
