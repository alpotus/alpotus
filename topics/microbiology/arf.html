ACUTE RHEUMATIC FEVER (ARF)

DEFINITION

Multisystem disease following group A streptococcal sore throat.
Occurs due to autoimmune reaction.
All manifestations resolve completely.
Exception → cardiac valvular damage → Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD).

ETIOLOGY

TABLE: | Factor | Details | | Organism | Group A Streptococcus (Streptococcus pyogenes) | | Source | Upper respiratory tract infection | | Serotypes | M types 1, 3, 5, 6, 14, 18, 19, 24, 27, 29 |

EPIDEMIOLOGY

  • Primary ARF → children (5–14 years)
  • Recurrent ARF → young adults
  • No gender association in ARF
  • RHD → more common in females

PATHOGENESIS

FLOWCHART: Group A streptococcal URTI -> M protein containing strains -> Host factors -> Genetic predisposition -> HLA-DR7 AND HLA-DR4 -> Immune response -> Antibodies against streptococcal M protein -> Molecular mimicry -> Cross-reaction with host tissues -> [Heart | Joints | Skin | Brain] -> Binding to valvular endothelium -> Inflammation -> Tissue damage -> Cardiac involvement -> Valvular damage -> Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD)

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Appear after ~3 weeks of streptococcal infection
  • Infection may be subclinical or sore throat

SYSTEM INVOLVEMENT

TABLE: | System | Manifestation | | Joints | Migratory polyarthritis | | Heart | Pancarditis | | Skin | Erythema marginatum, subcutaneous nodules | | CNS | Chorea (Sydenham) |

INDIVIDUAL FEATURES

  • Migratory polyarthritis
    → Most common manifestation
    → Joints hot, swollen, red, tender
    → Migrates joint to joint
    → Common joints: knees, ankles, hips, elbows
  • Pancarditis
    → Endocardium + myocardium + pericardium
    → Mnemonic: KYBAGS
  • Subcutaneous nodules
    → Painless, small, mobile
    → Over bony prominences (hands, feet, elbows)
  • Chorea (Sydenham)
    → Involuntary movements
    → Head and limb involvement
  • Erythema marginatum
    → Pink macular rash
    → Appears and disappears rapidly

DIAGNOSIS – JONES CRITERIA (2015)

BASIS

  • Combination of
  • Clinical features
  • ECG findings
  • Laboratory findings (ESR, CRP)
  • Evidence of preceding streptococcal infection required

MAJOR CRITERIA

TABLE: | Low-risk population | High-risk population | | Carditis (clinical/subclinical) | Carditis (clinical/subclinical) | | Polyarthritis | Monoarthritis or polyarthritis | | Chorea | Chorea | | Erythema marginatum | Erythema marginatum | | Subcutaneous nodules | Subcutaneous nodules | | — | Polyarthralgia |

MINOR CRITERIA

TABLE: | Low-risk | High-risk | | Polyarthralgia | Monoarthralgia | | Fever ≥ 38.5°C | Fever ≥ 38.0°C | | ESR ≥ 60 mm/h or CRP ≥ 3 mg/dL | ESR ≥ 30 mm/h or CRP ≥ 3 mg/dL | | Prolonged PR interval | Prolonged PR interval |

DIAGNOSTIC COMBINATION

TABLE: | Situation | Criteria | | Initial ARF | 2 major OR 1 major + 2 minor | | Recurrent ARF | 2 major OR 1 major + 2 minor OR 3 minor |

NOTE

  • Evidence of preceding streptococcal infection is essential.

LABORATORY DIAGNOSIS

  • Evidence of preceding streptococcal infection
TABLE: | Test | Details | | ASO titer | Elevated (>200 adults / >250 children), rising titer significant | | Anti-DNase B | Useful when ASO not elevated | | Throat culture | S. pyogenes, often negative later | | Rapid antigen test | Positive in recent infection |

Additional findings

TABLE: | Test | Finding | | ESR | Elevated | | CRP | Elevated | | Leukocytosis | Moderate (10,000–15,000) | | ECG | Prolonged PR interval |

ECHOCARDIOGRAPHY

  • Detects subclinical carditis
  • Identifies valvular regurgitation (mitral/aortic)
  • Shows leaflet thickening and annular dilatation
  • Helps differentiate from infective endocarditis

TREATMENT

  • Penicillin → drug of choice

REGIMEN

TABLE: | Route | Drug | Duration | | Oral | Penicillin V / Amoxicillin | 10 days | | IM | Benzathine penicillin G | Single dose (1.2 million units) |

SUPPORTIVE

  • Aspirin
  • → For arthritis, arthralgia, fever

PREVENTION

PRIMARY PREVENTION

  • Early treatment of streptococcal sore throat
  • Penicillin within 9 days
  • Prevents ARF

SECONDARY PREVENTION

  • Long-term prophylaxis required
TABLE: | Drug | Regimen | | Benzathine penicillin G | Every 4 weeks (IM) | | Alternative | Erythromycin 250 mg twice daily |

DURATION

TABLE: | Condition | Duration | | ARF without carditis | 5 years or till 21 years | | ARF with carditis, no residual disease | 10 years or till 21 years | | ARF with persistent valvular disease | 10 years or till 40 years or lifelong |
